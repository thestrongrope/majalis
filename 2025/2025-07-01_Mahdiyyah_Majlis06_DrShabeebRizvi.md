It is impossible for a person to truly recognize the Imam by merely relying on their own intellect to comprehend the reality of Imamate. Imamate is beyond human intellectual grasp.

#### The Divine Nature of Gnosis

Since human intellect cannot comprehend the reality of the Imam and Imamate, the question arises: how can a person recognize the Imam? The answer is that just as a person cannot understand the reality of Allah until Allah introduces Himself, similarly, a person cannot recognize the reality of the Imam until the Imam introduces himself. Until the Imam speaks about Imamate, it is impossible for people to understand what an Imam is and what Imamate entails. If they cannot understand the reality of the Imam and Imamate with their intellects, how can they choose an Imam?

No one can recognize Allah until Allah introduces Himself. A narrator once asked an Imam, "Do people have the ability to reach Gnosis, to perceive Gnosis? Do they have the ability, the capacity?" The Imam replied, "No, this is from Allah. Whomever Allah gives Gnosis, he will get Gnosis. Gnosis is only an act of the Lord God. People have no say in it."

If one questions the Hadith that says "acquire Gnosis," the answer lies in two important meanings:
1.  When Allah introduces Himself and makes Himself known, the servant should accept it.
2.  Providing the premises and preliminaries of this Gnosis is the servant's responsibility.

This question arises because opponents claim that after the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him and his holy family) departed, he left the religion in the hands of the people to select whomever they wished, which raises questions about the nature of such a religion.
