#### The Ignorance of the Arabs and True Inquiry

The speaker highlighted the profound ignorance of the Arabs of that time. Instead of asking profound questions about the heavens or divine knowledge, they would pose trivial questions like, "How many grains of sand are in the desert?" or "How many stars are in the sky?" Such questions held no true benefit.

It was primarily people of other religions, possessing a modicum of sense, who would approach Ali (peace be upon him) with meaningful inquiries. For instance, a Jew once asked someone sitting on a throne, "Where is Allah?" The response was, "Allah is above." The Jew immediately retorted, "If He is above, does that mean the earth is empty of Allah? He is not on earth?" The individual became angry and expelled the Jew, accusing him of defiling the mosque.

This incident, a well-known Hadith, describes how the Commander of the Faithful, Salman al-Farsi, then guided these curious individuals to Ali (peace be upon him). When they asked Ali the same question, "Where is Allah?", he responded, "The question is wrong." He explained that Allah created space and encompasses all space, so it is incorrect to ask "where" of Him. He created time and space, thus questions concerning time and space cannot be asked about Him. Allah encompasses all space, all time, and everything. Therefore, questions should be asked *about* Him, not *where* He is. In this manner, Ali (peace be upon him) answered many profound questions, leading these individuals to understand the unique position and knowledge of the Ahlul Bayt.

#### The Imam's Knowledge: `Hadith` and `Laylat al-Qadr`

The Quran itself is called `Hadith`, even `ahsanal Hadith` (the best of Hadith), meaning "the best new thing" or "the best new talk." `Hadith` refers to something that did not exist before but came into existence later. The Imam continuously explains his knowledge to the people, ensuring that the chain of religious teachings and the knowledge of the Ahlul Bayt never ceases. If it were to stop, the system of the world would cease.

The speaker advised that if one's prayers are unanswered, they should seek intercession through Lady Narjis, saying, "I ask you for the sake of your noble mother, Lady Narjis," as she made immense sacrifices.

She observed Lady Narjis showing no signs of pregnancy, leading to a momentary doubt. Imam Hasan al-Askari (peace be upon him) reassured her, "Aunty, do not fall into doubt. Allah's promise will surely be fulfilled this night."

The Imam informed them that the oppressor's work was finished and they should return. ### The Spirit of Sacrifice and Loyalty to the Imams

The essential condition for being close to the Imams is possessing the spirit of sacrifice. If one loves the world, it will eventually pass away, and such a person will inevitably abandon the Imam.

#### The Example of Abu Dharr al-Ghifari

Abu Dharr, a great companion of the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him and his holy family), could have easily gained worldly possessions if he had chosen to compromise. However, after the martyrdom of the Messenger, he would stand holding the handle of the Kaaba door and declare:

"O people, I am Abu Dharr. Whoever knows me, knows me, I am Abu Dharr. No Muslim can deny Abu Dharr's integrity or his status as a tremendous companion. Yet, during the era of the third ruler, when many Jews were prevalent and Abu Dharr would publicly rebuke wrongdoings, he became a "headache." The third ruler decided to send him to Syria, hoping the governor there would manage him.

Upon arriving in Syria, the governor, Muawiya, had built a magnificent palace. He invited everyone for its grand opening, including Abu Dharr, who arrived in simple clothes, holding a staff, amidst a crowd of well-dressed Muslims and Quran memorizers. Muawiya approached him and asked, "O companion of the Messenger of Allah, how do you like my palace?" Abu Dharr responded, "I am thinking." When asked what he was thinking, Abu Dharr directly asked, "Did I build this with my own money or with public money?" Muawiya remained silent. Abu Dharr then continued, "I will answer. "And if you built it with public money, then it is stolen property."

This public humiliation caused Muawiya to complain to Medina. Abu Dharr was then sent back. In Medina, he continued his rebukes. When Ka'b al-Ahbar, who had converted to Islam in the 9th year of Hijra, objected to Abu Dharr's "un-Islamic" statements, Abu Dharr struck him on the head with his staff, saying, "Hey, son of a Jew, will you teach me Islam?"

Deemed an unbearable problem, Abu Dharr was then exiled to Rabza, where he died in complete isolation and poverty. His wife, Umm Dharr, was crying as he was dying because they did not even have money for a shroud. Abu Dharr reassured her, recalling a Hadith from the Messenger of Allah (peace be upon him and his holy family): "O Abu Dharr, you will die in such a place that when there is no one, a few believers will come and they will arrange for your burial." They turned out to be believers who, upon learning it was Abu Dharr, provided him a proper burial. The Ahlul Bayt always make arrangements for their loyal companions.
