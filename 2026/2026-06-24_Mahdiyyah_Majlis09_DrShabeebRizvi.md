## Summary

The speaker discusses the Quranic call to race toward good deeds and rush to Allah's forgiveness and Paradise, which is prepared for the pious. According to Islamic traditions, the primary manifestation of piety (taqwa) is the Wilayah of Ali ibn Abi Talib (عليه السلام), without which true piety cannot exist. The pious are characterized by spending in all circumstances, controlling their anger, forgiving others, and seeking sincere repentance when they err. Sincere repentance requires not repeating the sin, as doing so is akin to mocking oneself. Furthermore, seeking forgiveness is most effective when utilizing the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) and his Ahl al-Bayt as a means (wasilah) to Allah, as highlighted in Surah an-Nisa. The speaker emphasizes that knowledge must be paired with action, citing the narration of Imam Ali (عليه السلام) that the perfection of religion lies in seeking knowledge and acting upon it.

The lecture highlights that Allah instills the love of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) and his visitation in the hearts of those for whom He desires good. ... Conversely, those who object to the mourning rituals (Azadari) of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) exhibit a lack of this love. The speaker stresses the vital importance of having deep spiritual recognition (ma'rifah) of the Imam of the time. Without it, people fall into misguidance, much like the Kharijites during the Battle of Siffin who forced Imam Ali (عليه السلام) to halt the battle because they lacked true recognition of him as the speaking Quran. The Ahl al-Bayt are the ultimate source of guidance, as they save humanity from misguidance, blindness, and ignorance. This is reflected in the exegesis of Surah al-Jinn, verse 16:

وَأَنْ لَوِ اسْتَقَامُوا عَلَى الطَّرِيقَةِ لَأَسْقَيْنَاهُمْ مَاءً غَدَقًا
> **Reference:** Quran, Surah al-Jinn 72:16

which the Ahl al-Bayt explain as remaining steadfast on the Wilayah of Ali ibn Abi Talib (عليه السلام) to receive their divine teachings.

The speaker underscores that the Wilayah of the Ahl al-Bayt is divinely ordained, pointing to Surah al-Ma'idah, verse 55, which restricts guardianship to Allah, His Messenger (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), and Imam Ali (عليه السلام) who gave charity while bowing in prayer. True leaders (Ulul Amr) are those upon whom angels descend during Laylat al-Qadr. To emphasize the gravity of following divinely appointed leaders, the speaker shares a highly authentic Hadith Qudsi narrated by Imam Muhammad al-Baqir (عليه السلام):

قَالَ اللهُ تَبَارَكَ وَتَعَالَى لَأُعَذِّبَنَّ كُلَّ رَعِيَّةٍ فِي الْإِسْلَامِ بِوَلَايَتِ كُلِّ إِمَامٍ جَائِرٍ لَيْسَ مِنَ اللهِ وَإِنْ كَانَتِ الرَّعِيَّةُ فِي أَعْمَالِهَا بَرَّةً تَقِيَّةً
> **Reference:** [Al-Kaafi](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01348/h/104874) (Shaikh Kulayni (a.r.), d. 329 AH), vol. 1, p. 376, H. 973 — Imam al-Baqir (عليه السلام) via Habib al-Sajistani; Hadith Qudsi

This narration warns that Allah will strictly punish any group that follows an unjust leader not appointed by Him, regardless of how righteous their outward deeds may appear. The divine appointment of the Ahl al-Bayt is clearly distinguished by their unparalleled knowledge, as demonstrated in the profound sermons of Nahj al-Balaghah by Imam Ali (عليه السلام).

Now he could not even lie because the Imam is well aware. How could he say no to the Imam? "When smiling faces fade, know that they have been remembered," you understand, right? It is absolutely clear. The Imam said, "Did you feel bad?" He said, "Yes, I felt bad." "Why did you feel bad? Tell me! Why did you feel bad?" He said, "O Ali, why..." The Imam said, "Twenty years ago—twenty years ago, yes! ## The Importance of Bara'at

He said, "But I had repented. I had repented." The Imam said, "You repented, but Allah did not accept your repentance. Why did Allah not accept your repentance? Because there was no *bara'at* (disassociation), right? There was no *bara'at* alongside *wilayat*, which is why Allah did not accept your repentance either." And that is why it is necessary for a person to have *bara'at*. Look, we do not say that you should go around shouting it everywhere. Right? We do not say this. Yes, if you are fed up with a miserable life, and there is no point in living anymore, then fine, do it, there is no issue. If you have to go, then go on that path. Otherwise, it will be like the example of the rooster. You remember the example of the rooster, right? Yes. That rooster used to give the call to prayer early in the morning, so he started giving the call to prayer early in the morning. The rooster gives the call to prayer in the morning, in the narration, in the hadith. So his owner said, "Why do you give the call to prayer early in the morning? My sleep is ruined." So he said, "Brother, Allah commanded me to give the call to prayer." He said, "No, my sleep is ruined."

That is, before telling this incident, let me tell you a little background. What is its background? Many riots occurred in Baghdad. There was only a 1% Shia population in Karkh. If they had stopped the very first time, nothing would have happened.

So in the same way, this rooster too. That is why I told you the background. When the rooster spoke, they said, "Brother, do not shout loudly. Give the call to prayer quietly." ## The Martyrdom of Ali Asghar

Now, hearing Hussain's words, the wretched once turned their faces away and began to weep. It was near that signs of revolution would appear when Ibn Sa'd called out, "Where is Harmala? Where is Harmala?" The cursed Harmala came forward. Ibn Sa'd said:

The arrow pierced through, and the other side of the arrow struck Hussain's arm. The child fluttered in his father's lap, and when Hussain looked down, there was a smile on Asghar's face.

Now Hussain wants to take Asghar back to the tents for the final farewell. As he approaches the tents, the thought comes to his mind, "I do not know if the mother will be able to bear this or not." Thinking this, Hussain turns back.

 سارعوا الی مغفرة

 الذین اذا ظلموا انفسهم جاؤکا
