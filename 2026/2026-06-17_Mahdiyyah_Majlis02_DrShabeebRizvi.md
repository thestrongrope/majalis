## Summary

The lecture centers on the Quranic command to "compete in good deeds" (فَاسْتَبِقُوا الْخَيْرَاتِ), emphasizing that all goodness is embodied in the personality of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام). Citing Imam Sadiq (عليه السلام), the speaker explains that Allah grants goodness to those He loves by placing the love of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) and his pilgrimage in their hearts. A significant portion of the discourse focuses on the nature of intellect (عقل), which the speaker describes as a divine light and the greatest blessing bestowed upon humanity. Drawing from traditions, the lecturer notes that intellect acts as a restraint—a "peg" or "stump"—that prevents the human soul from becoming an unruly, sinful beast.

The lecture further explores the characteristics of the intelligent person, highlighting that true intellect is demonstrated through consistency between one's words and actions. The speaker warns against the hypocrisy of commanding others to righteousness while neglecting one's own conduct, citing Quranic verses that condemn such behavior. By referencing the example of Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), who refrained from eating honey for three days before advising another to do the same, the lecturer illustrates that authentic wisdom requires personal practice. Ultimately, the speaker encourages the audience to seek this divine light through the Quran and the Ahlulbayt (عليهم السلام), as their teachings provide the essential guidance for intellectual and spiritual growth.

## The Farewell at Medina

On 28th Rajab, Hussain (عليه السلام) left Medina. Muhammad ibn Sinan Kufi narrates that by chance, I reached Medina on the same day that Hussain (عليه السلام) was leaving Medina. That same day, he was leaving Medina. I saw that sadness was cast all around. People are despondent. I asked, 'What kind of sadness is this?' People asked, 'Are you a stranger?' I said, 'I am from Kufa.' Then people said that the soul of Medina, the spirit of Medina, Hussain ibn Ali (عليه السلام), is leaving Medina. Muhammad ibn Sinan says that I thought, 'Let me also see how the people of Medina bid farewell to Hussain ibn Ali (عليه السلام).' Now, when I reached near the honorable house (Bayt al-Sharaf), I saw a large crowd gathered there. A big crowd, people were coming and watching how Hussain (عليه السلام) was leaving Medina.

### The Departure of the Sayyedahs

Just a little while later, two young men came out of Bayt al-Sharaf. They pushed the crowd away, saying, 'Move back! Move back!' The Sayyedahs are leaving, the Sayyedahs are about to mount! Everyone move back! The Prophet's granddaughters are about to mount! The crowd was pushed back, and one by one a camel arrived, and one by one a lady mounted it. He said, 'I saw another lady emerge. When she mounted, I saw a 13-year-old boy helping her to mount.' Very young. I asked, 'Who is this?' He said, 'This is the orphan of Hasan (عليه السلام), Qasim (عليه السلام), and he is helping his mother, Umm Farwah, to mount.'

Now, only a short while had passed when another dignified lady emerged. When she did, another young man stepped forward and helped her mount. I asked, 'Who is this?' He said, 'This is Hussain's (عليه السلام) son, the likeness of the Prophet, Ali Akbar (عليه السلام), and he is helping his mother, Umm Layla, to mount.' Now, a little later, another dignified lady emerged, and a tall, strong young man, muscular and robust, stepped forward and helped her mount. I asked, 'Who is this?' He said, 'This is Ali's (عليه السلام) younger daughter, Umm Kulthum (عليها السلام), and the one helping her mount is Abbas (عليه السلام), the Moon of Banu Hashim.'

Until now, when all the ladies were mounting, they were mounting on the palanquins. Hussain (عليه السلام) was sitting on a chair, and Hussain (عليه السلام) and...

Someone held her sandals, someone supported her arm. Someone removed the curtain of the gathering and helped the woman mount. I asked, "Who is this?" ## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- The Quranic verse "فَاسْتَبِقُوا الْخَيْرَاتِ" (compete in good deeds) serves as a divine call for believers to surpass one another in goodness.
- All goodness and good deeds are embodied in the personality of Imam Hussain ibn Ali (عليه السلام), whose love is a sign of Allah’s favor.
- The intellect (عقل) is described as a light created directly by Allah and is considered the greatest blessing bestowed upon human beings.
- The human soul (nafs) is likened to an unruly beast that requires the "peg" of intellect to prevent it from wandering into sin.
- The Quranic terms "أُولِي النُّهَى" (possessors of intellect) and "أُولِي الْأَلْبَابِ" (possessors of essence) highlight that true humanity is defined by the capacity to restrain oneself from evil.
- A person’s level of intellect is reflected in their ability to grasp information, with the highest level being those who understand a point before it is fully articulated.
- An intelligent person is defined by their ability to learn from experience, ensuring they do not stumble over the same obstacle twice.
- True intellect requires consistency between one's words and actions; preaching without practicing is a sign of hypocrisy and is detested by Allah.
- A wise person is practical and relies on their deeds, whereas an ignorant person relies on empty daydreams and excessive talk.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) taught that an intelligent person responds to the ignorance of others with wisdom and patience rather than retaliation.
