## Summary

The lecture begins with a narration from Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) highlighting the immense reward of shedding even a tiny tear out of grief for the Ahl al-Bayt, which can erase countless sins:
"یا فضیل، من ذکرنا او ذکرنا عندہ، فخرج عن عینیہ مثل جناح الذباب، غفر اللہ لہ ذنوبہ ولو کان اکثر من زبد البحر"
> Reference:
The speaker then discusses the advice given by Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) to a man named Khalid, who requested brief counsel. The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised him to remain detached from what is in people's hands, as this is true wealth, and to avoid greed, which is actual poverty. He also advised him to pray as if it were his farewell prayer:
"وصل صلۃ مودع"
> Reference:
Regarding prayer, the speaker emphasizes that true prayer must restrain a person from immorality and wrongdoing, as stated in the Quran:
"ان الصلوۃ تنھی عن الفحشاء والمنکر"
> Reference:
Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) explained that the acceptance and benefit of one's prayer are measured by how much it prevents them from committing sins:
"اعلم ان الصلوۃ حجۃ اللہ فی الارض"
> Reference:
"فمن احب ان یعلم ما ادرک من نفع صلاتہ علیھم السلام ورحمۃ اللہ، فلینظر فان کانت حجزتہ عن الفواحش والمنکر، فانما ادرک من نفعھا بقدر ما احتجز"
> Reference:
Furthermore, prayer requires deep humility. Imam al-Sajjad (عليه السلام) would change color out of awe when standing for prayer and would prostrate until he was drenched in sweat, contrasting with those who pray too quickly.

The Prophet's (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) next advice was to avoid actions that require an apology:
"وایاک وما تعتذر منہ"
> Reference:
The speaker notes that people often harm others with their tongues. Imam Ali (عليه السلام) taught that days are of two types: those in one's favor and those against. One should not be arrogant when dominating, nor despair when facing adversity:
"الیوم یومان، یوم لک و یوم علیک"
> Reference:
It is far easier to avoid committing sins than to seek repentance afterward:
"ترك الخطيئة أيسر من طلب التوبة"
> Reference:
A momentary desire can lead to lifelong grief:
"وكم من شهوة ساعة أورثت حزنا طويلا"
> Reference:
To illustrate this, the speaker shares the story of a man whose betrayal of trust twenty years prior led to his removal from the spiritual authority of the Ahl al-Bayt to that of their enemies, showing that superficial apologies are insufficient. Imam Ali (عليه السلام) also stated that death exposes the reality of this world:
"والموت فضح الدنيا"
> Reference:
When death arrives, the veil is lifted, as described in the Quran:
"لقد كنت في غفلة من هذا"
> Reference:
"فكشفنا عنك غطاءك فبصرك اليوم حديد"
> Reference:
The Quran commands believers to abandon all sins, whether open or secret:
"وذروا ظاهر الإثم وباطنه"
> Reference:
"إن الذين يكسبون الإثم سيجزون بما كانوا يقترفون"
> Reference:
Those who are engulfed by their sins are destined for the Fire eternally:
"بلى من كسب سيئة وأحاطت به خطيئته فأولئك أصحاب النار هم فيها خالدون"
> Reference:
Since only the Infallibles, prophets, and angels are free from sin, ordinary humans must repent immediately when they err. Sins against Allah require sincere repentance, while sins against people must be resolved by seeking forgiveness from the wronged party before death, as unresolved transgressions will prevent one from crossing the Sirat bridge.

## The Prophet's Five Advices

The series we were discussing was about the narration where a person named Khalid came to the Holy Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) — اللھم صل علی محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجھم والعن اعدائھم — and said, "O Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), advise me, but keep it brief so that I can remember it."

The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said, "Then I advise you with five things."

### Independence and the Danger of Greed

First, despair of whatever is in the hands of people. That is, never look at what others possess.

Second, and this is the present wealth. The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said this is the present wealth. That is, true wealth is that a person is not dependent on anyone and does not look back at anyone.

And, in another narration, it is found: 
واستغن عن الناس تکن۔۔۔
> Reference:

meaning, for example, become independent of people, and you will become their equal. No matter how great someone is, if you do not seek anything from them, you are their equal.

Then after that, he says:
وایاک والطمع فانہ الفقر، ففقر الحاضر۔
> Reference:

And see, you must stay away from greed, because this is actual poverty; "faqr" meaning actual poverty is this. A greedy person, because a greedy person is one whose stomach is never filled. And what is the example given for greed? It is like salty water. Salty water is such that when a person drinks it, no matter how much he keeps taking it, whatever he eats, his stomach is never filled.

### Praying the Farewell Prayer

And after that, he gives the third advice. The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) says:
وصل صلۃ مودع۔
> Reference:

And see, pray your prayer as if it is your farewell prayer.

About which I spoke in your service yesterday. Today, I will narrate another hadith in this regard, and then after that, there are the remaining two things, about which — وصلی اللہ علیہ والہ وسلام — we will discuss, God willing, if Allah grants the opportunity.

### Prayer as a Shield Against Sin

So when prayer was being discussed, there are two more important points. I will just present one hadith in your service, I will not take much of your time. If we pray, the effect of the prayer should be that it keeps us away from sins. The Quran says:
ان الصلوۃ تنھی عن الفحشاء والمنکر۔
> Reference:

Prayer keeps people away from indecency and evil. If our prayer does not do this, if our prayer does not perform this function, it means there is a problem somewhere in our prayer. There is some issue. Now we have to sort out what the problem is, where we are falling short. There cannot be a problem in the prayer itself; the problem can only be in us.

So look, a narration from Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) — اللھم صل علی محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجھم والعن اعدائھم اجمعین — says:
اعلم ان الصلوۃ حجۃ اللہ فی الارض،
> Reference:

"Know that prayer is Allah's restraint on earth." Look, prayer is a restraint of Allah on earth that stops people. "Hujzah" means that which stops something.
فمن احب ان یعلم ما ادرک من نفع صلاتہ علیھم السلام ورحمۃ اللہ،
> Reference:

"So whoever wishes to know what benefit he has gained from his prayer..." If he wants to know how much benefit he has derived from his prayer, how useful his prayer is, how beneficial his prayer is — meaning how much it has been accepted, in other words —
فلینظر فان کانت حجزتہ عن الفواحش والمنکر، فانما ادرک من نفعھا بقدر ما احتجز۔
> Reference:

"...then let him look: if it has restrained him from indecencies and evil, then he has indeed benefited from it to the extent that he was restrained." So he should look at how much his prayer is keeping him away from sins and evils. If prayer is not keeping him away from sins and evils, then it means something is wrong. To whatever extent prayer stops him from sins, to that extent his prayer is accepted, as the Imam (عليه السلام) says. To whatever extent.

To whatever extent. It is clear, isn't it? For example, let's say a person is praying, but he is not obeying his parents. His parents are telling him to do something. You know that for a recommended act, even — sorry, if it is a recommended prayer, and if parents call, one should even break the prayer. This is for recommended prayer, not for obligatory prayer. But for recommended prayer, if...

And if they call out fully, then leave everything, it's over. If the father says, "Son, come here," and instead your fear and humility in prayer increases — no, no, that does not work. That does not work. Before that, if the parents have spoken, then it means it is over. See how much importance Allah is giving: "Leave My presence and go to them." Have you reflected on this? "Leave My presence and go." So other recommended things have no value at all. Even prayer, which is the main pillar of religion — as I presented to you from the hadith about the importance of prayer — prayer is going into the presence of Allah, yet there Allah says, "No, if your father calls you, leave My presence and go to your father, serve him; go to your mother, serve her." That is the importance. Those who are half-baked scholars try to justify otherwise, but that has no value. So this is an important thing.

It is found in a narration that Imam al-Sajjad (عليه السلام) — اللھم صل علی محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجھم۔ Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) says, speaking about his grandfather:

When he stood for prayer,

تغیر لونو۔
> Reference:

His color would change.

Now, how do we pray? Extremely fast! We go into prostration so quickly that even the angels cannot count; before one quota is finished, we move to the next, immediately. In front of the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), a man prayed like this, and the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said that if he prays in this manner, he will not die on my religion — meaning he will not die as a Muslim if he prays like this. ### Avoiding Deeds That Require Apology

And that is why, look, the next advice the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) gave was:
وایاک وما تعتذر منہ۔
> Reference:

And see, do not do any such deed for which you have to apologize. Always, before doing any work — any action, whether of the hands and feet or even of the tongue — we often hurt people with our tongue, don't we? We speak of ourselves; whatever bad there is, we first apply it to ourselves, not to others. First of all, when we hurt someone, we hurt them through our tongue. So:
ایاک و ما تعتذر منہ۔
> Reference:

Do not do any deed that causes pain to the other person. Always put yourself in their shoes and see: if someone were to speak to us like this, how would it feel? How would we feel if we are doing this to them and they did the same to us? And a person should never think, "Oh, this won't happen to us, our case is different." No, no, all these thoughts in the mind are satanic whispers, yes, the whispers of Satan. No one knows when Allah might change someone's circumstances.

Today is fine, today Allah has made you dominate over them, but tomorrow it is possible that circumstances will change. Commander of the Faithful (عليه السلام) says that days are of two kinds:
الیوم یومان، یوم لک و یوم علیک،
> Reference:

Days are of two kinds: one day is for you, and one day is against you. That is, one is in your favor. The day that is in your favor, which is in your right, the day you are dominating, do not be arrogant on that day. Do not show pride. And the days that are going against you, when everything is suppressed, you are oppressed, you are being crushed, do not despair of Allah's mercy on that day. Look at the teachings, look at the teachings of Commander of the Faithful (عليه السلام). Therefore, a person should never think, "No, no, nothing will happen to us." That is why, look in the narrations, so that you do not have to apologize. In the narrations, you see, just as the chapter on prayer is very vast, similarly, this chapter on sins is also very vast. When a person does not commit a sin, he is 100% safe, which means he has not done it. But if he commits a sin and then repents, it is not certain whether his repentance will be accepted or not.

## The Difficulty of Repentance vs. Avoiding Sin

You ask, as has been said, and you just say a simple "sorry" to them. Just saying sorry from the tongue is not necessarily accepted by the heart. It is possible that in their heart—Assalamu Alaykum—there is such a wound in their heart that they can never express. This wound of the heart—Assalamu—the wound of the heart is never forgiven. Allah will hold one accountable for it. Therefore, now look at the narration, I present to you, please recite a Salawat upon Muhammad and his family, [اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم]. The narration is from Hazrat Amir (عليه السلام), who says:

"ترك الخطيئة أيسر من طلب التوبة"
> Reference:

That is, leaving the mistake, leaving the sin, leaving the error is easier. It is much easier compared to you seeking repentance, regretting, and apologizing. So, between the two, this is where the ease lies.

"وكم من شهوة ساعة"

Sometimes a moment's desire, meaning when Satan completely takes over, takes the human into his grip, Satan takes the human into his grip. A moment's desire,

leaves them grieving for a long time. A brief moment of desire. A desire of a few minutes. That is, in Arabic, when 'sa'ah' (ساعة) is used, 'sa'ah' does not mean exactly one minute, two minutes, or one hour. It means a portion of time. Now, that could be two minutes, five minutes, or an hour, depending on the context in which it is used. So, a short-lived desire. And we see its example in this world. ## Weighing Our Actions Like Gold

Think about it, meaning a person should think ten times before doing any action. They should think a lot: "Brother, what are we doing?" Every single action, meaning, in other words, in simple terms, make your every single action very valuable. Valuable means, before doing it, whether it is through the tongue, through thought, or through hands and feet. Weigh it heavily, even your thoughts. Weigh it, measure where this thing will lead. Think. What will be its effect?

Look, the goldsmith, you have seen a goldsmith, right? When a goldsmith weighs, if you observe, he places such tiny weights and then looks. Because gold is a valuable thing. Right? But on the other hand, if you go to buy wheat, what does he do? He puts a one-kilogram weight, then throws it in, places it, and even gives you a little extra to make you happy. He says, "Here, a handful more." You are happy, no problem. But a goldsmith is not like that. Because wheat does not have that value. Wheat does not have that value. But gold is very valuable. يا فضيل، من ذكرنا او ذكرنا عندہ، فخرج عن عینیہ مثل جناح الذباب، غفر اللہ لہ ذنوبہ ولو کان اکثر من زبد البحر
> Reference:
explaining that if someone sheds even a tiny tear the size of a fly's wing when they remember or hear about the Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام), Allah will forgive all of their sins, even if they are as abundant as the foam of the sea.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised Khalid to despair of what is in the hands of other people, explaining that this detachment is true, present wealth and makes a person equal to others.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) warned against greed, describing it as actual poverty and comparing it to salty water, which only increases a person's thirst the more they drink it.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised performing every prayer as if it were a farewell prayer, and Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) explained that a prayer is only beneficial and accepted to the extent that it restrains a person from committing sins and evil deeds.
- Allah places such immense importance on the rights of parents that if a parent calls their child while they are performing a recommended (mustahab) prayer, the child must break their prayer to answer them.
- True prayer requires deep humility and sincerity; when Imam al-Sajjad (عليه السلام) stood for prayer, his color would change, and he would remain in prostration until he was drenched in sweat, whereas praying too quickly without sincerity is condemned.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised avoiding actions and words that would force a person to apologize later, emphasizing that one should always consider how their words might hurt others.
- Imam Ali (عليه السلام) taught that avoiding sins is far easier than seeking repentance, stating:
ترك الخطيئة أيسر من طلب التوبة

and warned that a brief moment of worldly desire can result in long-lasting grief.
- It is highly recommended to bring joy to fellow believers and strictly forbidden to cause them grief, as Imam al-Baqir (عليه السلام) stated that smiling in the face of a brother is a good deed [political commentary omitted] and that there is no worship more beloved to Allah than bringing joy to believers.
