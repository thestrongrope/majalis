## Summary

The lecture emphasizes the critical importance of recognizing the Ahl al-Bayt (عليه السلام) as the source of all goodness, referencing the Quranic command:
فاستبقوا الخیرات
The speaker explains that true success and entry into paradise are impossible without the recognition (ma'rifah) of the Imam of one's time. This is underscored by the famous prophetic tradition:
من مات ولم یعرف امام زمانہ مات میتۃ الجاہلیۃ
According to Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام), dying a "death of ignorance" (jahiliyyah) does not merely mean dying without knowledge, but rather dying in disbelief, misguidance, and hypocrisy. To attain this essential recognition, the speaker highlights the immense value of Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah and Ziyarat al-Ghadiriyyah, both gifted by Imam Ali al-Naqi (عليه السلام). Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah, along with Salat al-Layl and Ziyarat Ashura, was highly recommended by Imam al-Zaman (عليه السلام) and was compiled by Sheikh al-Saduq in his highly reliable work *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih*.

The speaker further discusses the unique and incomparable status of the Ahl al-Bayt (عليه السلام). Interpreting the final verse of Surah al-Kahf:
فمن کان یرجو لقاء ربہ فلیعمل عملاً صالحاً ولا یشرک بعبادۃ ربہ احدا
the speaker explains that "good deeds" refer to accepting the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام), and "not associating partners" means not associating anyone else in his Wilayah. Even highly esteemed figures like Umm Salamah (عليها السلام), despite her immense courage and defense of Wilayah, were not permitted under the Cloak (Kisa) because they did not possess this infallible status. Highlighting their cosmic role, the speaker quotes a narration from Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) describing the Ahl al-Bayt (عليه السلام) as the leaders of Muslims, the proofs of Allah over the worlds, and the safety for the inhabitants of the earth:
نحن ائمۃ المسلمین وحجۃ اللہ علی العالمین وسادۃ المؤمنین وقادۃ الغر المحجلین وموالی المؤمنین نحن امان اہل الارض کما ان النجوم امان لاہل السماء
The Imam (عليه السلام) further explains that they are the means through whom Allah sustains the heavens and the earth, prevents earthquakes, and sends down rain:
نحن الذین بما بنا یمسک اللہ السماء ان تقع علی الارض الا باذن وَبِنَا یُمْسِکُ الْاَرْضَ اَنْ تَمِیْدَ بِاَہْلِہَا وَبِنَا یُنْزِلُ الْغَیْثَ
Without the presence of a divine Proof (Hujjah), whether manifest or hidden, the earth would perish:
لَوْلَا الْحُجَّۃُ لَسَاخَتِ الْاَرْضُ بِاَہْلِہَا
When asked about the benefit of a hidden Imam, Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) compared it to the sun benefiting the earth even when obscured by clouds.

Finally, the lecture outlines a tradition from the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) regarding three groups of people who are divinely protected from Iblis and his hosts:
ثَلَاثَۃٌ مَعْصُوْمُوْنَ مِنْ اِبْلِیْسَ وَجُنُوْدِہٖ
The first group consists of those who constantly remember Allah:
اَلذَّاکِرُوْنَ لِلّٰہِ
Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) explains that true remembrance is not merely verbal recitation, but the difficult task of evaluating every action to ensure it aligns with Allah's permissions. The second group consists of those who weep out of fear of Allah:
اَلْبَاکُوْنَ مِنْ خَشْیَۃِ اللہ
This is exemplified by the emotional supplications of the Ahl al-Bayt (عليه السلام), such as Dua Kumayl and the intense weeping of Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) during prostration. The third group consists of those who seek forgiveness at dawn:
والمستغفرون بالاسحار
The speaker emphasizes that seeking forgiveness during the pre-dawn hours (sahar) requires individuals to actively recall and repent for their past sins rather than trying to forget them.

## The Concept of Good Deeds and Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام)

I had submitted to you that *khayrat* is the plural of *khayr* (good), and the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام) are the source and the entirety of all good.

نحن اصل و کل خیر واعدائنا اصل و کل شر

> **Reference:** [Al-Kaafi](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01348/h/119033) (Shaikh Kulayni (a.r.), d. 329 AH), vol. 8, pp. 242–243, H. 15132 — Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام)

We are the source of every good, we are the root of every good, and our enemies are the root of every evil. Our enemies are the root of every evil.

I have been continuously submitting to you that the recognition (*ma'rifah*) of this good is very essential. That is, the recognition of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام) is very essential, and you must have heard this hadith many times:

من مات ولم یعرف امام زمانہ مات میتۃ الجاہلیۃ

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/232553) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.), d. 1110 AH), vol. 23, p. 75, H. 11616 — Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) via Al-Mahaasin (al-Barqi)

And as long as there is no recognition of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام), salvation is not possible for a human being. Salvation means that entering Paradise is simply not possible for them.

This is the saying of the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم). The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said:

من مات ولم یعرف امام زمانہ مات میتۃ الجاہلیۃ

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/232553) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.), d. 1110 AH), vol. 23, p. 75, H. 11616 — Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) via Al-Mahaasin (al-Barqi)

He died the death of ignorance (*jahiliyyah*).

### Defining the Death of Ignorance

So when someone asked Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) what *jahiliyyah* means, how do people usually translate *jahiliyyah*? Ignorance (*jahalat*). That is an incorrect translation. It is not the correct translation; it is completely wrong. There is a difference between *jahiliyyah* and *jahalat*, and even the Imam clarified this, the Imam also explained it.

The Imam said that *jahiliyyah* means disbelief (*kufr*) and hypocrisy (*nifaq*). Disbelief, polytheism (*shirk*), and hypocrisy; disbelief and deviation; disbelief, misguidance (*dalal*), and hypocrisy—three things. The Imam said:

میتۃ کفرا و ضلال و نفاق

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/232582) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 23, pp. 86–87, H. 11645 — Imam al-Baqir (عليه السلام) via Al-Mahaasin (al-Barqi)

That is, whoever dies without recognizing their Imam dies a death of disbelief, meaning they will not die in Islam; they will die in misguidance and they will die a death of hypocrisy.

That is, outwardly they might be a Muslim, outwardly they might recite the *kalimah*, but actually they will not die as a believer; rather, they will die as a hypocrite. So this is a very important thing, which is why the recognition of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام) is so essential, and no matter how much we discuss it, it is never enough. Look at this, Imam Ali al-Naqi (عليه السلام) [اللہم صلی علی محمد و آل محمد وعجل فرجھم] has done two great favors for us. One great favor is by expounding *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah*, and the second great favor is *Ziyarat al-Ghadiriyyah*—the *Ziyarat* of Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) for the day of Ghadir, which is narrated from Imam Ali al-Naqi (عليه السلام). And for all of us, it is incumbent and necessary upon all of us to recite these two *Ziyarats*.

## The Significance of Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah

As far as *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah* is concerned, this is the command of the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام) himself.

When the Imam met with the Baghdadi, he commanded him with three things. First, *Salat al-Layl*—the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام) told him to be punctual with the night prayer. When he was blessed with that honor, the Imam commanded him with three things: first, *Salat al-Layl*; second, *Ziyarat Ashura*; and third, *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah*. The Imam mentioned these three things. *Salat al-Layl*, in any case, is the best thing in terms of worship. That is to say, the night prayer is the crown of all worship. The night prayer is the crown of all worship, and if a person starts their morning with the night prayer and then prays the morning prayer, their day will go wonderfully. Divine support, assistance, and divine assistance will come. He will receive help and support from Allah.

*Ziyarat Ashura* is related to Imam al-Husayn (عليه السلام), which is the best thing concerning Imam al-Husayn (عليه السلام). And the third, *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah*, is the best thing for the recognition of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام). It is the best *Ziyarat*, in which you will actually understand the status and the station of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام).

The *Ziyarat*—the hadith—that I am about to present to you now... because you know, who was the first scholar of hadith (*muhaddith*) who narrated *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah*? Shaykh al-Saduq. Shaykh al-Saduq (رضوان اللہ تعالیٰ علیہ). You know that 'Saduq' was not his name; 'Saduq' was his title. *Saduq* means one who speaks the truth excessively. One who speaks the truth a great deal is called *Saduq*, which is a form of exaggeration (*sighah al-mubalaghah*) in Arabic. Is that clear to you?

His name was Muhammad ibn Ali ibn al-Husayn ibn Musa ibn Babawayh al-Qummi (رضوان اللہ تعالیٰ علیہ).

Shaykh al-Saduq, who was a very great scholar of hadith, had the title *Ra'is al-Muhaddithin* (Chief of the Muhaddithin), and his father was also a very great scholar of hadith, Ali ibn al-Husayn ibn Musa ibn Babawayh. He narrated this hadith and this *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah* in *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih*. *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih* is his book. You know, right? I am explaining for the children what *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih* means. When you say *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih*, what does it mean? It means: 'For him who does not have a jurist (*faqih*) present.' This book is sufficient for someone who does not have a jurist in their midst. So if someone is completely alone, for example, if a person is alone on an island and has no one to guide them, they are alone somewhere and only have *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih* with them, then this is sufficient for them. Shaykh al-Saduq wrote this in his book and gave it this title. It is clear, right? That for whoever has *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih*—*la yahduruhu* means 'is not present'—the jurist is not present, so this book is sufficient for them. And what else? In the preface of this book, he writes that in this book, he has only narrated those hadiths about which he had 100% faith that they are authentic. There is not a single hadith in it about which he had even the slightest doubt. This was his level of confidence. So, Shaykh al-Saduq narrated this hadith in his books *Kamal al-Din* and *al-Amali*—these are two of his books—and its phrases are very similar to the phrases of *Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah*. So, I present this narration to you. This narration is reported from Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام). Salawat.

اللهم صل علی محمد و آل محمد و عجل فرجہم و لعن اعدائہم اجمعین

We will read the sentences slowly, and you should continue to understand the status of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام), continue to understand their station. Because if we understand these things, then realize that they cannot be compared to anyone; that is, no human being, other than these Infallibles (علیہم السلام), can equal them—we cannot even imagine it. And that is why, before I present the next sentence to your noble presence and then begin reading the hadith, in our narrations, there is the last verse of Surah al-Kahf. The last verse of Surah al-Kahf is:

اللهم صل علی محمد و آل محمد و عجل فرجہم و لعن اعدائہم اجمعین

> **Reference:** Quran, Surah al-Kahf 18:110

فمن کان یرجو لقاء ربہ

So whoever hopes for the meeting with his Lord—*falyamal amalan saliha*—he should do righteous deeds, *wala yushrik bi-ibadati rabbihi ahada*—and not associate anyone in the worship of his Lord, not make anyone a partner. It is present in the exegesis of this verse that:

فمن کان یرجو لقاء ربہ فلیعمل عملاً صالحاً

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/234339) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 24, pp. 377–378, H. 13402 — Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) via Abi Baseer, from Tafsir al-Qummi

He must do good deeds, which means he must accept the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام). Wah wah, Subhan Allah!

He must accept the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام), and *wala yushrik bi-ibadati rabbihi ahada*—and not associate anyone in the worship of his Lord, meaning he should not associate anyone else in the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام). Wah wah, Subhan Allah!

In the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام), there are only and exclusively the 11 Infallibles, the 11 Imams (علیہم السلام) after Ali (عليه السلام)—meaning a total of 14 Infallibles (علیہم السلام). Apart from them, no one else can even be conceived of. Lady Umm Salamah (عليها السلام) has a very high status, a very high status indeed. Recently, a very voluminous book has also been written about her, and Alhamdulillah, its translation has also been completed, thanks be to Allah. Editing is currently underway, and Insha'Allah, you will see it published in English very soon. Umm al-Mu'minin Umm Salamah (سلام اللہ علیہا)—but even when she requested permission from the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) in the Hadith of the Cloak (*Hadith al-Kisa*) to enter under the cloak, as is mentioned in the Hadith of the Cloak, the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) did not give her permission either. He said: "Indeed, you are upon good; you are good, but you are not worthy of this." Wah wah, wah wah!

You are good, certainly you are very good, there is no issue with that, but you are not suited for this cloak—meaning you are not at this level, you are not of this status to be here. Look, let me submit to you, she is the Mother of the Believers (*Umm al-Mu'minin*), and truly a defender of Ali (عليه السلام), defending the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام), and she defended the Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام) with great courage. After the martyrdom of the Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), Lady Umm Salamah (عليها السلام) defended it extensively. Truly, the reality is that she has a great station, a great status. Indeed, for her status, it is sufficient that Imam al-Husayn (عليه السلام) used to call her "Mother". Have you pondered upon this? I mean, what could be a greater status than this? Therefore, it is very essential that we understand the station of the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام). Now, look at this narration that I present to you. It is present in *Kamal al-Din* of Shaykh al-Saduq, and also in *al-Amali*, which is also by Shaykh al-Saduq. We are narrating from it that the fourth Imam, Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) says:

## The Status of the Imams (علیہم السلام)

He says:

نحن ائمۃ المسلمین

> **Reference:** [Al-Amaali](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/02560/h/439629) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), Majlis 34, vol. 1, p. 186, H. 278 — Imam Zayn al-Abideen (عليه السلام) via al-Aamash

We are the Imams of the Muslims. There is no other Imam. There is no thirteenth Imam besides the twelve. Right? Therefore, do not call anyone else "Imam"; the Imams are only twelve. Indeed. Right? There is no thirteenth Imam. The Imam says: "We are the Imams of the Muslims, only us." There is no one besides them. Therefore, think carefully before using words: what are we saying? What are we saying, and what will be its consequences? Because we will have to answer for it. Do not think that we can say anything and get away with it, thinking, "Who will ask? We have support." No, no, that is worldly support. Once you enter the grave, you will be asked there too: "Who is your Imam?" Wah wah wah!

So there, just as the Master of the Universe (عليه السلام) said to Lady Fatima bint Asad (عليها السلام), "Your son, your son, your son, your son." When Lady Fatima bint Asad (عليها السلام) was asked in the grave, "Who is your Imam?" the Master of the Universe (عليه السلام) said, "Your son, your son," or perhaps the Noble Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said, "Your son," the Noble Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said:

اللهم صل علی محمد و آل محمد و عجل فرجہم و لعن اعدائہم اجمعین

Meaning, "Your son, your son." So the Imams are only twelve; there is no Imam besides them. You will be asked in the grave, and if you go there and take some third or fourth name, then understand that the trouble will start from there and continue until the Day of Judgment. Then what will happen on the Day of Judgment? Allah knows best. After that, he says:

وحجۃ اللہ علی العالمین

> **Reference:** [Al-Amaali](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/02560/h/439629) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), Majlis 34, vol. 1, p. 186, H. 278 — Imam Zayn al-Abideen (عليه السلام) via al-Aamash

And we, the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام), are the proof (*Hujjah*) of Allah over the entire universe, over all the worlds.

و سادۃ المؤمنین

> **Reference:** [Al-Amaali](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/02560/h/439629) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), Majlis 34, vol. 1, p. 186, H. 278 — Imam Zayn al-Abideen (عليه السلام) via al-Aamash

## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- The Imams (عليه السلام) are the origin and entirety of all good, while their enemies are the root of all evil.
- Knowing the Imam of one's time is essential for salvation, as dying without this recognition is a death of ignorance, which Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) explained as dying in disbelief, misguidance, and hypocrisy:
من مات ولم یعرف امام زمانہ مات میتۃ الجاہلیۃ
- Imam Ali al-Naqi (عليه السلام) bestowed two great favors upon the believers: Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah and Ziyarat al-Ghadiriyyah.
- Imam al-Zaman (عليه السلام) commanded three essential practices: the Night Prayer (Salat al-Layl), Ziyarat Ashura, and Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah.
- Sheikh al-Saduq, whose real name is Muhammad bin Ali bin Husayn bin Musa bin Babawayh Qummi, recorded Ziyarat al-Jamiah al-Kabirah in his book *Man La Yahduruhu al-Faqih*, which was compiled to serve as a sufficient guide for a believer when no jurist is present.
- The Quranic verse commanding believers to perform righteous deeds and not associate partners in worship is interpreted as accepting the Wilayah of Imam Ali (عليه السلام) and not associating anyone else in his Wilayah:
فمن کان یرجو لقاء ربہ فلیعمل عملاً صالحاً ولا یشرک بعبادۃ ربہ احدا
- Imam Zayn al-Abidin (عليه السلام) described the status of the Imams (عليه السلام) as the leaders of Muslims, the proofs of Allah, and the source of safety for the earth:
نحن ائمۃ المسلمین
- Prophet Muhammad (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) stated that three groups are protected from Iblis and his forces: those who remember Allah constantly, those who weep out of fear of Allah, and those who seek forgiveness at dawn:
ثَلَاثَۃٌ مَعْصُوْمُوْنَ مِنْ اِبْلِیْسَ وَجُنُوْدِہٖ
- Seeking forgiveness (Istighfar), especially at dawn, brings continuous heavenly bounties, wealth, children, gardens, and rivers, as Prophet Noah (عليه السلام) promised his people:
فقلت استغفروا ربکم انہ کان غفارا یرسل السماء علیکم مدرارا ویمددکم باموال وبنین ویجعل لکم جنات ویجعل لکم انھارا
- On the Day of Resurrection, Allah will remove punishment from three groups of people, including those who are pleased with Allah's decree and those who are well-wishers for Muslims.
