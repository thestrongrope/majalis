## Summary

The lecture begins by highlighting a Hadith from Imam Ja'far ibn Muhammad (عليه السلام), in which he assures that whoever remembers the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام) and sheds even a tear as small as a fly’s wing out of sorrow for them, Allah will forgive all their sins, regardless of their quantity. The speaker addresses those who object to this by limiting the scope of Allah’s mercy, explaining that such skepticism stems from a lack of *Ma'rifat* (cognition). By referencing the Quranic account of the Children of Israel being promised forgiveness for entering a gate with prostration and the word "Hittah," the speaker emphasizes that the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام) are the purpose of creation and that Allah’s grace is infinite, far exceeding the limited, material understanding of those who fail to recognize their status.

The discourse then shifts to the dangers of greed, citing the advice given by the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) and Imam al-Kazim (عليه السلام) to Hisham ibn Hakam. Greed is described as "present poverty" and a "key to disgrace" that robs a person of their intellect, chivalry, and honor. The speaker encourages believers to abandon expectations from worldly people and instead turn to the threshold of Imam al-Zaman (عليه السلام), the Master of Blessings. By maintaining independence from worldly flattery and focusing on the spiritual guidance provided by the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام), a believer preserves their dignity and true knowledge, as demonstrated by the lives of the Imams' (عليه السلام) noble companions and those who remained steadfast in their principles.

## The Virtue of Remembering the Ahlul Bayt
This Hadith Shareef has been chosen for this discourse. In this Hadith, Imam Sadiq (عليه السلام) addresses Janab Fudhail ibn Yasar, saying: "Oh Fudhail, whoever remembers us, or if we are remembered in his presence, and from his eyes, a single drop of tear flows, even if it is as small as the wing of a fly – just one drop – if that much tear flows, Allah will forgive all his sins, all of them. Even if his sins are as numerous as the foam of the sea."

The foam of the sea, the ocean foam, even if sins are more than that, Allah will forgive them. Sometimes people object, saying that this means you are encouraging sin, suggesting that if you sin, Allah will forgive you, if you cry, He will forgive you. How can Allah just forgive? These are the people who want to limit Allah’s mercy. They want to limit Allah's mercy according to their own understanding. They think Allah's mercy will be just as we imagine it. But Allah's mercy is not like that. Allah's mercy is very vast, very expansive. Allah forgives whoever He wills. He bestows His grace as much as He wants. There is no limit to Allah’s grace. In His grace and His mercy, He is infinite. He is infinite even in His infinity. He is so benevolent, and He has stated this in the Quran.

Let me give an example. People object, saying that if one merely cries for Imam Hussain (عليه السلام), Allah will forgive their sins. Look, what is the problem with the Quran? These people do not read the Quran. If they read the Quran, then they do not pay attention to it.

There is a verse in the Holy Quran, not just one, but this point is mentioned in two places: Allah said that We told the Children of Israel to enter this city through its gate and just keep saying: (قولوا حطة نغفر لكم خطيئاتكم) "Say 'Hittah' [remission] and We will forgive you your sins." Just go, go into prostration and say "Hittah," and We will forgive all your sins, all of them.

Imam (عليه السلام) explains in the tafseer (exegesis) of this verse that it was a wooden door. For that, Allah said, "Just prostrate and enter," and Allah was ready to forgive all their sins.

So these are the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام). They are the purpose of creation, the goal of creation, for whom Allah created the entire universe. If someone sheds a single tear in their sorrow, in their remembrance, and Allah forgives all their sins, what is there to be surprised about?

For instance, if someone, for example, a common person like us, for him, 200, 500, or a thousand rupees is a big deal. A thousand rupees is a big deal. ## Advice of the Prophet and the Companions of the Imams
So what we were discussing regarding the Hadith, the discussion was that a man named Khalid came to the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) and said,  يا رسول الله أوصني (O Messenger of Allah, advise me.) So the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) gave him five pieces of advice. We were discussing the second piece of advice:  إياك والطمع (Beware of greed.) See, do not be greedy,  فإنه الفقر الحاضر (for it is the present poverty.) What is greed? It is "present poverty," actual existing poverty, destitution. Greed, no matter how great a person may be, if there is greed in his heart, he will be poor. Meaning, even with money, he is poor. And one who is not greedy, even if he has nothing, he is still wealthy. He is still wealthy. But this greed has many effects. Meaning, it has many effects.

So if you see, we present a narration in your service. From the seventh Imam, Imam al-Kazim (عليه السلام). اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم وأهلك أعدائهم أجمعين.

The Imam (عليه السلام) to his companion Hisham ibn Hakam. You know, Hisham ibn Hakam was a very great personality. Look, recognize the companions of the Imams (عليه السلام). For us, it's very important to have knowledge of the Imams (عليه السلام), firstly. Of course, first knowledge of Allah, knowledge of the Messenger (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), and then knowledge of the Imams (عليه السلام). This is very important. After that, even recognize their companions. So that you see what kind of companions the Imams (عليه السلام) had. It's very important. Hisham ibn Hakam is a truly remarkable personality. Just imagine, he didn't even have a beard yet. He was hardly a certain age. He must have been 16-17 years old.

And at that age, when a Syrian man came, from Syria, to debate the Imam (عليه السلام) in Mecca during Hajj. So many great, influential individuals were there. Great companions of the Imam (عليه السلام). One after another. Meaning, the Imam (عليه السلام) tells Yunus to go and bring all their debaters, the Shia debaters. So Yunus goes and brings them. Many people, four, five, six names are mentioned in the Hadith. He brings them. But the Imam (عليه السلام), despite seeing all of them, says, "Not these, I want Hisham." I want Hisham, and it's a phrase from the Hadith itself: Hisham was very young at that time, hardly 16-17 years old. But look how much the Imam (عليه السلام) desires Hisham. The sixth Imam (عليه السلام), the sixth Imam (عليه السلام).

And then, when that Syrian starts debating, the Imam (عليه السلام) tells Hisham, "Now you answer him." And when Hisham starts answering, that Syrian has no reply. He completely silences him. At such a young age. And then the Imam (عليه السلام) asks Hisham, "Hisham, when you went to Basra, how did you conduct that debate?" So Hisham narrates the entire debate. After that, the Imam (عليه السلام) asks, "Hisham, where did you learn this debating from?" So Hisham replies, "Master, everything I've learned, I've learned from your threshold." "You taught me. Besides that, I know nothing." So Hisham, the Master, hearing this, says, "Hisham, by Allah, the debate you conducted, it is exactly as it is found in the divine books."

Now look, these are young boys, they debate, but what is it? He is so close to the Imam (عليه السلام), so close, that he completely absorbs the Imam's (عليه السلام) culture. Now Imam al-Kazim (عليه السلام) advises Hisham:  يا هشام إياك والطمع (O Hisham, beware of greed.) See, never be greedy for the world.  وعليك باليأس مما في أيدي الناس (And you must despair of what is in the hands of people.) Never expect anything from what people possess. The Imam (عليه السلام) says:  وامت الطمع من المخلوقين (And kill greed for what is with the created beings.) Whatever your expectation from people is, kill it. "Amit" means give it death. Kill it. If there is even a slight expectation from someone. So what will this poor one give? The Giver is Allah, the Imam (عليه السلام). What will this poor one give? Kill it, the Imam (عليه السلام) says:  امت الطمع من المخلوقين (Kill greed for what is with the created beings.) Ponder over the phrase. Meaning, kill greed. Give death to expectation, don't keep any.  فإن الطمع مفتاح للذل (For greed is the key to disgrace.) Because this greed, this expectation from people, it is the key, it is the key for disgrace. It is the key to humiliation and ignominy.

## The Consequences Of Greed
And what else do they say? This thing takes away intellect, and `اخْتِلَافِ الْمُرُوَّه` (deviation from chivalry/manliness). That is, `مُرُوَّت` (chivalry), `مُرُوَّت` means, a person's, what do you call it, chivalry; in English, they say, all the noble virtues that exist, this destroys them. All the noble virtues a person has, all his good qualities, this greed obliterates them, destroys them.

And then the Imam (عليه السلام) says: `وَتُدْنِيَ سَفَلَ الْعِرْضِ` (and lowers the base level of honor). This thing takes away a person's honor. Honor is lost. The freshness of one's face is gone. Greed is such a thing. The freshness of one's face is gone. Honor is lost. `وَذَهَابُ بِالْعِلْمِ` (and the departure of knowledge). ## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- Imam Ja'far ibn Muhammad (عليه السلام) stated that whoever remembers the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام) and sheds a tear as small as a fly's wing will have all their sins forgiven, regardless of their quantity.
- The mercy of Allah is infinite and expansive, and objections to the forgiveness granted through the remembrance of the Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام) stem from a lack of Ma'rifat (recognition).
- The Ahlul Bayt (عليه السلام) are the purpose of creation, and as stated by Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام), they are the creations of Allah, while the rest of creation was created by them.
- Greed is described as "present poverty," and the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised that one must kill greed for what is in the hands of others, as it is the key to disgrace.
- Greed is a destructive force that steals one's intellect, destroys noble virtues (chivalry/manliness), and leads to the loss of honor and knowledge.
- Imam al-Kazim (عليه السلام) emphasized that one should seek blessings from the threshold of the Imam of our time (عليه السلام), the Wali al-Ni'mah (Master of Blessings), rather than relying on worldly people.
- The Ziyarat Jami'a al-Kabirah, taught by the tenth Imam (عليه السلام), is a vital resource for understanding the true concept and magnificent nature of Imamate.
- Greed manifests in four branches: haughtiness (farah), arrogance (marah), obstinacy (lajajah), and vying for increase (takathur), all of which are discouraged or condemned in the Quran and by the Imams (عليه السلام).
- Worldly life is characterized by play, amusement, and boastful competition, but these things are fleeting and ultimately lead to regret, whereas true value lies in the knowledge bestowed by Allah.
