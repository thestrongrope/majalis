## Summary

The lecture begins with an exposition of the Quranic command "فاستبقوا الخيرات", which appears in Surah al-Baqarah and Surah al-Ma'idah, urging believers to compete with one another in performing good deeds ("خيرات"). The speaker clarifies that "خير" refers to any action that brings a person closer to Allah and Paradise. According to a hadith of Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام), the ultimate source of all good is the love of Husain (عليه السلام) and the love of visiting him, which Allah instills in the hearts of those for whom He wills good ("من أراد الله به الخير... قذف في قلبه حب الحسين وحب زيارته"). Conversely, when Allah intends evil for someone, He instills malice towards Husain (عليه السلام) and his visitation in their heart ("وَمَنْ أَرَادَ بِهِ الشَّرَّ قَذَفَ فِي قَلْبِهِ بُغْضَ الْحُسَيْنِ وَبُغْضَ زِيَارَتِهِ").

The speaker emphasizes that gatherings of mourning for the Ahlul Bayt (عليهم السلام) are immense blessings where all goodness converges. Those who constantly remember Husain (عليه السلام) are referred to in narrations as "حُدَّاثُ الْحُسَيْنِ". These gatherings are described as the ideal places to receive the divine spiritual breezes mentioned in a Hadith Qudsi, where Allah tells Prophet David (عَلَى نَبِيِّنَا وَآلِهِ وَعَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ): "يَا دَاوُدُ إِنَّ لِلَّهِ فِي أَيَّامِ دَهْرِكُمْ نَفَحَاتٍ أَلَا فَتَرَصَّدُوا بِهَا". The speaker highlights that participating in these rituals and spending in the way of Husain (عليه السلام) is a divine gift from Allah, who holds absolute sovereignty ("قُلِ اللَّهُمَّ مَالِكَ الْمُلْكِ..."). Furthermore, referencing a narration of Khaithamah, the speaker notes that the Imam (عليه السلام) urged Shias to meet and discuss their traditions to keep their cause alive, praying: "رَحِمَ اللهُ عَبْداً أَحْيَا أَمْرَنَا". The speaker asserts that without the recognition of the Imam (عليه السلام), practical deeds like prayers and fasting are of no use.

Finally, the lecture introduces a historical narration concerning the brave Yemeni companions from the tribe of Hamdan, such as Haris al-Hamdani, Kumayl ibn Ziyad, and Malik al-Ashtar. Having pledged allegiance during the lifetime of the Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), these courageous warriors sought to identify his successor. Upon seeing Ali (عليه السلام) enter, they immediately trembled, wept, and recognized him, declaring "هَذَا هُوَ!" and finding peace in their hearts. The speaker notes that their inherent bravery aligned with their love for the chief of the brave, Ali (عليه السلام).

## Competing in Good Deeds

I have been blessed with the opportunity to recite this noble verse. In this noble verse, Allah Almighty is saying: "فاستبقوا الخيرات". This phrase appears twice in the Quran, meaning this sentence, this phrase has occurred twice. One is in Surah al-Baqarah, verse 148, and the other is in Surah al-Ma'idah, verse 48. In Surah al-Baqarah it is verse 148, and in Surah al-Ma'idah it is verse 48. In both places, Allah says: "فاستبقوا الخيرات". "فاستبقوا" means that you compete, all of you, compete with one another in "خيرات" (good deeds). In Urdu, when we generally hear the word "خيرات" (khairat), it is understood as charity. But in Arabic, charity is called "صدقة" (sadaqah). In Arabic, "خيرات" is the plural of "خير" (khair/good). Compete with one another in good deeds. Try to excel and go ahead of each other. See that if someone is doing more, you should try to do even more than them. In this way, there should be a competition, but it should be in good deeds. Of course, there should be no jealousy (حسد) in this, where one tries to pull the other down or prays for the destruction of the other's bounties. That is called jealousy (حسد), and that is not the topic here. This means: "فاستبقوا الخيرات" — that is, try to excel and get ahead of one another in good deeds. This is the command of the Holy Quran.

### Hastening Towards Forgiveness

And these very "خيرات", these good deeds, what do they become the cause of? They lead to man receiving forgiveness from Allah, Allah forgives his sins. And if someone tries to go further and further ahead in "خيرات", Allah finds different excuses to forgive him. The Quran says: "وسارعوا إلى مغفرة من ربكم وجنة عرضها السماوات والأرض".

That is, all of you, run with haste. Look, in the first verse it was "فاستبقوا الخيرات" (compete). Now here it is "وسارعوا" (haste/hurry), meaning go with speed. In Arabic, "سرعة" means speed. With speed, go. "وسارعوا إلى مغفرة من ربكم" — hasten towards forgiveness from your Lord. And where else should you go? "وجنة" — towards that Paradise. Towards that Paradise whose width is like the distance between the heavens and the earth. And for whom has Allah prepared it? "أعدت للمتقين" — Allah has prepared this Paradise for the pious (people of Taqwa). Those in whose hearts is the fear of Allah.

## Defining the Concept of Khair

Now, here we will discuss two words, and then we will move the talk forward. Two things: first, what is "خير"?

"خير" means every good thing, everything that brings a person closer to Allah. That is called "خير". Every single thing that makes a person worthy of Paradise. Not worthy in the sense of deserving on one's own, because no one can deserve Paradise except by Allah's mercy on them, but it means that which takes one closer to Paradise. So, "خير" is everything that brings a person closer to Paradise, which becomes a cause for Allah's forgiveness upon him, for Allah's mercy upon him, so that he may enter Paradise. That is called "خير".

### The Ultimate Khair: Love of Imam Husain (عليه السلام)

Now the question arises, "خير" is many things. But is there any such "خير" that is the focus or the sum of all "خير"? Where all "خير" come together? In other words, if this "خير" is not there, then the rest of "خير" is useless. There must be some such "خير" that is the source, the fountain of all "خير".

So when we started searching the hadiths, or rather, started digging into them, to see what the hadiths say about "خير", we found a hadith of Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام).

Pay attention to the words of the hadith: "من أراد الله به الخير..." In Arabic, those who are familiar with Arabic know that when "الـ" (al-) comes in Arabic, it means the complete "خير", everything. This is called "استغراق" (encompassment) in Arabic terms. That is, all, however much "خير" there is. When Allah wills for someone all "خير", the complete "خير", the entirety of "خير" in which there is no exception, when Allah wills such "خير" for someone that nothing of "خير" is left out, "قذف في قلبه حب الحسين وحب زيارته".

Nara-e-Haidari! Ya Ali!Allah instills the love of Husain (عليه السلام) in his heart, and the love of visiting him.

وَمَنْ أَرَادَ بِهِ الْخَيْرَ...

And what is the opposite of *khair* (good)? It is *sharr* (evil). *Sharr* means evil, and *khair* means goodness. Now, when Allah intends evil for someone, what does evil mean? It means Allah leaves him to his own devices. Allah leaves him to his own devices, as in the Hadith:

لَا أُبَالِي فِي أَيِّ وَادٍ هَلَكَ

This means Allah says, "I do not care in which valley he perishes." Where he goes and dies, gets ruined, Allah says, "I do not care about him at all."

وَمَنْ أَرَادَ بِهِ الشَّرَّ قَذَفَ فِي قَلْبِهِ بُغْضَ الْحُسَيْنِ وَبُغْضَ زِيَارَتِهِ

Allah instills malice towards Husain (عليه السلام) and malice towards visiting him—meaning hatred—in his heart. He hates Husain (عليه السلام) and also hates visiting him.

## Seeking the Divine Breezes

Therefore, there is a Hadith Qudsi—what is a Hadith Qudsi? For children, we say: a Hadith Qudsi is that which Allah revealed to the Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), but it is not part of the Quran. It is called Hadith Qudsi. It is not part of the Quran. Allah revealed it, but not as the Quran. The Quran is a different thing. Allah revealed the Quran as a miracle. Therefore, the Quran is distinct, and Hadith Qudsi is distinct. These are two different things, but it is also the Word of Allah. So, the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) says that in a Hadith Qudsi, Allah addressed His Prophet, Prophet David (عَلَى نَبِيِّنَا وَآلِهِ وَعَلَيْهِ السَّلَامُ) and said:

يَا دَاوُدُ إِنَّ لِلَّهِ فِي أَيَّامِ دَهْرِكُمْ نَفَحَاتٍ أَلَا فَتَرَصَّدُوا بِهَا

> **Note:** Prophetic hadith — cited in commentary in [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407), vol. 74, p. 166; not recorded as a standalone isnad entry in the Imami hadith corpus.

"O David, know that in your times, there are spiritual breezes from Allah. Spiritual breezes from Allah blow. Therefore,"

فَتَرَصَّدُوا بِهَا

"Therefore, keep yourselves in such a place where you can catch them." *Tarassadu* comes from *mirsad*, *rasad*, and *rasda*. *Rasda* in Arabic, *mirsad* means where, you know, an animal waits for its prey, like a lion. If a lion is waiting for its prey, it sits in a place knowing that the prey is going to pass by here. So, it sits in complete silence, so that as soon as the prey passes, it will immediately attack. This is called *mirsad* in Arabic. There is also a Quranic verse:

إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ

Meaning: "Indeed, your Lord is ever watchful."

إِنَّ رَبَّكَ لَبِالْمِرْصَادِ

Here, in this Hadith Qudsi, Allah is saying: *fatarassadu biha*—keep yourselves in a place where these divine breezes are blowing. These divine breezes... and there can be no better place than where the Ahlul Bayt (عليهم السلام) are remembered.

## The Visit of Khaithamah and Reviving the Cause

This is why the Imam (عليه السلام) said—a famous narration, regarding Khaithamah, a very famous narration that we have read many times—that Khaithamah is a lover of the Imam (عليه السلام). He comes to Medina after performing the Hajj to meet the Imam (عليه السلام), because you know that the Hajj is not complete without visiting the Imam (عليه السلام).

Indeed.

In today's times, if someone goes for Hajj, it is necessary for them to go to Medina. And in Medina, they should visit the Imams of Baqi (عليهم السلام), meaning read their Ziyarah, read the Ziyarah of the Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم), and read the Ziyarah of Janab-e-Zahra (عليها السلام).

And after that, of course, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام) at all times, not forgetting him, constantly. Meaning, whoever is going for Hajj, if they are performing Tawaf, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام) there. If they are performing Sa'i between Safa and Marwah, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام). When they are going to Muzdalifah, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام). When they are going to Mina, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام). When they are going to Arafat, they should remember the Imam of our Time (عليه السلام). If they are on Mount of Mercy (Jabal al-Rahmah), they should remember the Imam (عليه السلام) there. In short, in every state and at every place, they should keep remembering the Imam (عليه السلام).In every situation, one should continue to remember one's Imam, and after that, in any case, coming to Medina is necessary. So, Khaisama comes to the Imam after the Hajj and meets him to say goodbye. After that, Khaisama says, "Master, now I am going back to my homeland. Is there any message for the Shias? Is there any message for the Shias?"

The Imam replied, "Khaisama, أَبْلِغْ مَوَالِيَنَا السَّلَامَ. Convey our salam to our Shias, and tell them to keep meeting one another in their homes and keep discussing our traditions (ahadith). Debate our traditions, discuss our traditions, because through this, our cause is kept alive."

And then the Imam prayed, "رَحِمَ اللهُ عَبْداً أَحْيَا أَمْرَنَا. May Allah bestow mercy upon the person who keeps our cause alive."

In a narration, it is mentioned that he asked, "كَيْفَ يُحْيَا أَمْرُكُمْ؟ How is your cause revived?" The Imam replied, "يَتَعَلَّمُ عُلُومَنَا وَيُعَلِّمُهَا النَّاسَ. He should learn our traditions and teach them to others. This is how our cause is kept alive."

And in another hadith, the Imam says, "It is not only our cause that is kept alive; the Imam says, 'He has kept us alive.' 'رَحِمَ اللهُ عَبْداً أَحْيَانَا'... 'أَحْيَانَا'. He has given us life, who recites our traditions, who reads our traditions."

There are extraordinary narrations regarding this. So these are all those places where Allah's spiritual breezes blow. Allah's breezes, and receiving them here is our responsibility, it is our job, and there can be no better place than this. «وَيَقُولُ: هُوَ الْيَوْمُ الَّذِي قُتِلَ فِيهِ الْحُسَيْنُ (صَلَّى اللهُ عَلَيْهِ وَآلِهِ)»
'And he would say: This is the day in which Hussain (صلى الله عليه وآله) was killed.'

> **Reference:** [al-Amali](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/02560/h/439553) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), Majlis 27, vol. 1, p. 128, H. 202 — Imam al-Rida (عليه السلام) via Ibrahim b. Abi Mahmud

Yes, O mourners! These are the same days, the days of weeping and mourning, which have now arrived. Today is the beginning. Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) has entered our homes. Hussain (عليه السلام) is our guest.

## The Summoning of Imam Hussain to the Court

On the 28th of Rajab, this caravan set out from there. But before that, on the 22nd of Rajab, when the news of the death of the tyrant of Sham arrived, Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) was summoned to the court. He was summoned to the court. Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) was in the mosque at that time, and they came and told him, 'You have been summoned to the court.' Hussain (عليه السلام) came home and informed Janab-e-Zainab (عليها السلام), 'Zainab, I have been summoned to the court.'

Hearing this, Janab-e-Zainab (عليها السلام) became anxious. 'Brother, the court does not assemble at this hour of the night. Why have you been summoned so late at night?' He said, 'Sister, I know why I have been summoned. Their ruler has died, and I know what they will demand of me. They will ask for allegiance, which I can never give.' Janab-e-Zainab (عليها السلام) understood. She said, 'Brother, if you are going to the court at this hour, I will not let you go alone. O my brother Abbas (عليه السلام)! O my son Ali al-Akbar (عليه السلام)! Go and gather the youths of Bani Hashim!'...take them with you and go with the master to the court. O mourners! It is written in the books of martyrdom that there were thirty youths of Bani Hashim who surrounded Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) in their circle, and taking him in their circle, they proceeded towards Dar al-Imarah. As soon as they reached Dar al-Imarah, Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) said, "Now you all stay here; it is not appropriate for you to come inside. I will go alone. Yes, but if my voice is raised, then you all enter inside without permission, you all enter inside." 

### The Refusal of Allegiance to Yazid

So, he came, Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) was seated, and the news of the death of Muawiyah ibn Abi Sufyan was conveyed to him. Upon hearing the news, the Master said:

إنا لله وإنا إليه راجعون

After that, Walid read Yazid's letter, in which it was written: "O Walid, take the allegiance from Hussain."

Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) said, "This matter of allegiance is not something to be decided in the darkness of the night. Tomorrow morning, summon the Muslims, let everyone gather, and after that, it will be decided whose allegiance will be given and who will give allegiance."

Walid said, "No, Hussain! Hussain, you cannot leave like this. Yazid has insisted."

Hussain (عليه السلام) said, "No, this matter is not like that, and if you insist, then hear my decision:

مثلي لا يبايع مثله

A person like me can never pledge allegiance to a person like Yazid." And saying this, Hussain (عليه السلام) walked away.

### The Confrontation and the Intervention of Hazrat Abbas (عليه السلام)

As soon as Hussain (عليه السلام) left, Marwan called out, "Walid! Stop Hussain right now! Take the allegiance; if he does not pledge allegiance, then behead him at this very moment!"

As soon as the beloved of Fatimah (عليها السلام) heard this, he looked towards Marwan and said, "Who will kill me? You? Or Walid?

أتهددني بالقتل يا ابن الزرقاء؟

O son of a depraved woman! Do you threaten me with death?"

As soon as Hussain's (عليه السلام) voice was raised, suddenly the youths of Bani Hashim entered the court! Leading them was Hazrat Abul Fazl al-Abbas (عليه السلام) with a sword in hand. "Master! Who said what? Who dared?"

Hussain (عليه السلام) said, "Abbas! Abbas, this is not the time for war, Abbas, be patient." Hussain (عليه السلام) stopped Abbas. He urged Abbas (عليه السلام) to be patient.

I will say, O Master! This urging of patience continued until the Day of Ashura, and Abbas (عليه السلام) left this world with this very patience.

## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- The Quranic phrase "فاستبقوا الخيرات" appears twice in the Quran: in Surah al-Baqarah, verse 148, and Surah al-Ma'idah, verse 48.
- In Arabic, "خيرات" is the plural of "خير" (good deeds), whereas charity is called "صدقة".
- According to a hadith of Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام), when Allah wills complete "الْخَيْرَ" (goodness) for someone, He instills the love of Husain (عليه السلام) and the love of visiting him in their heart.
- When Allah intends "الشَّرَّ" (evil) for someone, He instills malice towards Husain (عليه السلام) and malice towards visiting him in their heart.
- Those who constantly remember and mention Husain (عليه السلام) are referred to in the narrations as *Huddaath al-Husain* (حُدَّاثُ الْحُسَيْنِ).
- Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) instructed Haris al-Hamdani to teach certain wisdoms to his children to increase their intellect, foresight (حزم), and judgment.
- When asked "ما العقل؟" (What is intellect?) by Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام), Imam Hasan al-Mujtaba (عليه السلام) defined it as "حفظ قلبك ما استودعته".
- When asked "فما الحزم؟" (What is foresight?), Imam Hasan (عليه السلام) defined it as "أن تنتظر فرصتك وتعاجل ما أمكنك".
