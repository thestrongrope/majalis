## Summary

The lecture emphasizes the importance of striving to excel in good deeds, noting that true competition in righteousness involves surpassing others in virtue rather than hindering them. The speaker highlights Sayyed-us-Shuhada, Imam Hussain (عليه السلام), as the embodiment of goodness and urges the audience to maintain a constant, year-round remembrance of him. This includes incorporating his name into daily prayers, particularly during the final prostration, as a means of acknowledging that the survival of the faith is owed to his sacrifice.

The lecture also explores the development of intellect and sound judgment, drawing upon the wisdom shared by Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) with his sons. The speaker explains that intellect is an instinct that must be cultivated through authentic knowledge — derived exclusively from the Quran and the teachings of the Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام) — and through diligent reflection upon one's life experiences. By maintaining high quality in all actions and practising prudence by waiting for the right opportunities, a believer can strengthen their decision-making. The lecture concludes by stressing that the quality of one's actions is a direct reflection of the depth of one's intellect.

## Striving in Good Deeds and the Remembrance of Imam Hussain

We had the honor of reciting this noble verse for the majlis. In it, Almighty Allah says: "فَاسْتَبِقُوا الْخَيْرَاتِ" (So strive to outdo each other in good deeds). Striving to precede does not mean trying to pull others down, but rather trying to go further than what others are doing, to do better than them.

As was mentioned in the previous two majalis, the embodiment of goodness is Sayyed-us-Shuhada, Imam Hussain (عليه السلام). And this remembrance of Hussain should not be limited only to the days of mourning — educate and nurture your children such that this remembrance is throughout the year. Rather, it should be daily. If possible, even in your prayers, remember Sayyed-us-Shuhada. In the last prostration of any prayer — be it morning, Dhuhr, Asr, or even a recommended prayer — remember Imam Hussain. The wisdom behind this is that today this prayer is alive because of Hussain; otherwise, religion would have ended.

There is a small phrase in the Ziyarat narrated in hadith: when one visits the grave of Sayyed-us-Shuhada, one prostrates upon the grave and recites:

اللهم رب الحسين بحق الحسين اشف صدر الحسين بظهور الحجة

O Allah, Lord of Hussain, by the right of Hussain, heal the chest of Hussain with the appearance of the Hujjah. Even this short phrase, recited daily in prayer, keeps the remembrance of Hussain alive and carries immense effect in one's life, family, and society.

> **Reference:** [Kamil al-Ziyarat](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01526) (Ibn Qulawayh (a.r.), d. 368 AH) — chapter on the actions at the grave of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام): "ثُمَّ تَضَعُ خَدَّيْكَ عَلَيْهِ وَتَقُولُ: اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ الْحُسَيْنِ اشْفِ صَدْرَ الْحُسَيْنِ، اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ الْحُسَيْنِ اطْلُبْ بِدَمِ الْحُسَيْنِ..."

اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم والعن أعدائهم أجمعين

## Intellect, Wisdom, and Human Agency

The discussion was ongoing about intellect and wisdom. The questions that Mawla-e-Kaenat, Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام), asked Imam Hassan (عليه السلام) and Imam Hussain (عليه السلام), and the answers they gave — after those answers, Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) said to Harith Hamdani:
يا حارث علّموا هذه الحكم أولادكم
O Harith, teach these wisdoms to your children, because:
فَإِنَّهَا زِيَادَةٌ فِي الْعَقْلِ وَالْحَزْمِ وَالرَّأْيِ
it increases intellect, foresight, and sound judgment.

> **Reference:** [Maani al-Akhbar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01560/h/372233) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), vol. 1, p. 401, H. 833 · [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/269266) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 75, pp. 101–102, H. 48351

Sound judgment — *raay* — means making correct decisions. Because one very important thing in a person's life is making correct decisions.

Allah has made humans free-willed and powerful. He made some things obligatory, some recommended, some forbidden, some disliked, and the rest permissible — these five are the *Ahkam-e-Khamsa*. In these five categories, Allah has kept humans completely free; no one intervenes, not even Allah Himself. This is the highlight of the Shia Ithna Ashari faith. Some people say Allah compels us (the Mujabbira — Ash'ari, Salafi); others say Allah cannot intervene (the Mufawwizah — Mu'tazila). The Imāmī position is the middle: neither compulsion nor complete delegation, but rather *amr bayna amrayn*.

The actual decision in any matter belongs exclusively to the person making it. That is why Mawla says: your decision-making prowess will be very strong if you know these things.

So the question arises: how to increase intellect? Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) captured it in very few words — the standard of eloquence is to say profound things in fewer words:
الْعَقْلُ غَرِيزَةٌ تَزِيدُ بِالْعِلْمِ وَالتَّجَارِبِ
Intellect is an instinct. Allah gives this instinct to everyone. But if someone wants to increase it, there are two things: knowledge and experience.

> **Reference:** [Uyun al-Hikam wa al-Mawa'iz](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01404/h/151781) (al-Laithi al-Wasiti (a.r.)), vol. 1, p. 52, H. 1346

Now what is knowledge? Knowledge means Quran and Hadith. Besides this, nothing else is knowledge in the true sense — wherever there is correct knowledge, its source ultimately traces to the Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام). The Imams say:
شَرِّقُوا أَوْ غَرِّبُوا
You may go to the East or to the West —
لَا تَجِدَانِ عِلْمًا صَحِيحًا إِلَّا مَا خَرَجَ مِنْ عِنْدِنَا أَهْلَ الْبَيْتِ
you will not find true knowledge anywhere except what has emanated from us, the Ahl al-Bayt.

> **Reference:** [Awaalem al-Uloom](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/00978/h/67331) (al-Bahrani (a.r.)), vol. 19, p. 410–411, H. 5388 — citing Al-Kaafi (Shaikh Kulaini (a.r.)); Imam al-Baqir (عليه السلام) to Salama ibn Kuhayl and al-Hakam ibn Utayba

The Master of Existence (عليه السلام) described everything in two words:
يا كميل، لا تأخذ إلا عنا تكن منا
O Kumayl, learn only from us — take knowledge from us and you will become one of us.

> **Reference:** [Tuhaf al-Uqool](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01440/h/303699) (Ibn Shu'ba al-Harrani (a.r.)), p. 171, H. 216 — wasiyya of Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) to Kumayl ibn Ziyad

And if you want to know where to go, the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said:
أَنَا مَدِينَةُ الْعِلْمِ وَعَلِيٌّ بَابُهَا
فَمَنْ أَرَادَ الْمَدِينَةَ وَالْحِكْمَةَ فَلْيَأْتِهَا مِنْ بَابِهَا
I am the city of knowledge and Ali is its gate; whoever wants the city of knowledge and wisdom, let him come through its gate.

> **Reference:** [Al-Amaali of Shaikh Saduq (a.r.)](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/02560/h/440186) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), p. 526, H. 873 — narrated as "أَنَا مَدِينَةُ الْحِكْمَةِ وَعَلِيٌّ بَابُهَا" (city of wisdom); the "مَدِينَةُ الْعِلْمِ" (city of knowledge) variant is also widely transmitted.

## The Importance of Quranic Study

That is why Maula says: one is knowledge — the Quran and the teachings of the Ahl al-Bayt. Read the Quran. We recommend in every majlis: make it compulsory that daily you recite at least 50 verses. Plan so that the entire Quran is completed within a target period. At an average of 150 verses per juz, 50 verses daily means one juz completed every three days. Read its translation, and then from a teacher familiar with hadith, read the tafsir of the Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام) — not tafsir from here and there. Otherwise, if you go here and there, you will reach here and there.

The Master of Existence (عليه السلام) said:
يا كميل، لا تأخذ إلا عنا تكن منا
O Kumayl, learn only from us; take knowledge from us and you will become one of us.

> **Reference:** [Tuhaf al-Uqool](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01440/h/303699) (Ibn Shu'ba al-Harrani (a.r.)), p. 171, H. 216

اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم

## Learning From Experience

The second thing is experience. Experience increases intellect in everyone's life. But the condition, as mentioned previously, is that one should write down their experiences and review them. Write down important experiences in life and revise them daily, or at least once a week — individual experience, collective experience, whatever it is. A believer is not deceived twice:
لَا يُلْسَعُ الْمُؤْمِنُ مِنْ جُحْرٍ مَرَّتَيْنِ
A believer is not bitten from the same hole twice. If you read *hijr*, it means stone; if you read *juhur*, it means a pit. He will not fall into the same pit twice. This is the sign of a believer. If someone deceives you a second time for the same reason, understand there is some problem with your faith.

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/259296) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 64, p. 362, H. 38381

(Sometimes you act out of pity, knowing you are being made a fool of but choosing to overlook it — that is not deception but mercy. The wrong thing is to be caught unexpectedly.)

As much as possible, acquire knowledge of the Quran and Hadith, learn them and excel in them.

## Quality of Action and Intellect

Whatever work you do, the Ahlulbayt (عليهم السلام) have taught us very good things. Unfortunately, we do not go to their door. Going to their door does not just mean visiting their shrine — though that too is good. It means learning their teachings and acting upon them, reviving their commands and their hadith. The Ahlulbayt (عليهم السلام) have given everything; it just needs someone to take it.

Mawla-e-Kaenat (عليه السلام) says:
كَيْفِيَّةُ الْفِعْلِ تَدُلُّ عَلَى كَمِيَّةِ الْعَقْلِ
The quality of the act indicates the quantity of intellect. The quality of your actions — everything in life, every work you do — shows how much intellect a person has. If he messes things up, his intellect is somewhat less. But if he takes responsibility for even the smallest work and does it well, records it and follows up on every detail, he will excel. Doing things superficially — just quickly glancing over — leaves out the inner meaning and causes loss.

> **Reference:** [Uyun al-Hikam wa al-Mawa'iz](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01404/h/157121) (al-Wasiti (a.r.)), vol. 1, p. 395, H. 6686

اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم
حیدری یا علی

## Prudence and Foresight

Mawla-e-Kaenat (عليه السلام) asked a second question to Imam al-Hasan (عليه السلام): "فما الحزم" (So what is prudence?). Imam al-Hasan (عليه السلام) replied:
أَنْ تَنْتَظِرَ فُرْصَتَكَ وَتُعَاجِلَ مَا أَمْكَنَكَ
That you wait for your opportunity, and hasten to do whatever you can. Keep waiting and watching for opportunities — whether for religion or for the world. A businessman always looks for the best opportunity to get maximum benefit; similarly, every person, if intelligent, will try to take maximum advantage within the permissible conditions.

So wait for your opportunity. And whatever power and ability you have to act, bring maximum and quick effort to bear — no procrastination. If you can do it, go ahead and do it.

> **Reference:** [Maani al-Akhbar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01560/h/372233) (Shaikh Saduq (a.r.), d. 381 AH), vol. 1, p. 401, H. 833 · [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/269266) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 75, pp. 101–102, H. 48351

The meaning of prudence also includes deliberation — it is not that you act without thinking. It is mentioned in narrations that Imam Ja'far al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) narrates that a person came into the presence of the Messenger of Allah (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) and said, "O Messenger of Allah, advise me." The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) asked him three times: "If I advise you, will you obey my words?" Each time he replied: "نعم يا رسول الله." Then the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) told him:
فَإِنِّي أُوصِيكَ إِذَا هَمَمْتَ بِأَمْرٍ فَتَدَبَّرَ
When you intend to do something, ponder over it.
فَإِنْ يَكُنْ رُشْدًا فَامْضِ وَإِنْ يَكُنْ غَيًّا فَانْتَهِ عَنْهُ
If it is guidance, then proceed; and if it is misguidance, then refrain from it.

> **Reference:** [Al-Mahasin](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01544/h/362567) (al-Barqi (a.r.)), vol. 1, p. 16, H. 46 — Imam al-Baqir (عليه السلام) citing the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم): "إِذَا هَمَمْتَ بِأَمْرٍ فَتَدَبَّرَ عَاقِبَتَهُ فَإِنْ يَكُ خَيْراً وَرُشْداً فَاتَّبِعْهُ وَإِنْ يَكُ غَيّاً فَدَعْهُ"

## The Importance of Reading Islamic Literature

You see, we again request children, youth, young adults: read Nahj al-Balagha and Ghurar al-Hikam. Sayyid Radhi (رضوان الله تعالى عليه), who compiled Nahj al-Balagha, passed away at only 47 years old — born in 359 AH, died in 406 AH — and yet see what an amazing work he produced. Read it; even translations are fine if you don't know Arabic. And there is the book "غرر الحكم و درر الكلم" (Pearls of Wisdom and Gems of Speech) compiled by Shaikh Abdul Wahid al-Amudi (a.r.) — 10,760 traditions from Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) alone, on so many subjects, mostly three or four words each. Read it.

Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) says:
مَنْ تَوَرَّطَ فِي الْاُمُورِ بِغَيْرِ نَظَرٍ فِي الْعَوَاقِبِ فَقَدْ تَعَرَّضَ لِلنَّوَائِبِ
If a person plunges into matters without thinking, without thorough consideration of consequences, they put themselves in difficulties. Therefore, planning is very important.

> **Reference:** [Tuhaf al-Uqool](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01440/h/303689) (Ibn Shu'ba al-Harrani (a.r.)), p. 90, H. 206 — wasiyya of Amir al-Mu'minin (عليه السلام) to Imam Hasan (عليه السلام)

## Foresight and the Purpose of Creation

The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) teaches us to reflect: if there is guidance in a matter, do it; if not, don't do it. People generally understand this as being about what to eat and drink, where to go and come from. These are minor matters (*juz'iyyat*) and don't carry that much importance — though one should be careful about them too. But here, the main topic is that for which we were created — are we being far-sighted about that? Are we thinking about it?

Mawla Ali (عليه السلام) says:
خُلِقْتُمْ لِلْبَقَاءِ وَمَا خُلِقْتُمْ لِلْفَنَاءِ
You were created for eternal existence, not for annihilation.

> **Reference:** Ghurar al-Hikam wa Durar al-Kalim (al-Amudi (a.r.), d. 510 AH), p. 272, no. 4

خُلِقْتُمْ لِلْآخِرَةِ وَمَا خُلِقْتُمْ لِلدُّنْيَا
You were created for the hereafter; you were not created for this world.

> **Reference:** Ghurar al-Hikam wa Durar al-Kalim (al-Amudi (a.r.), d. 510 AH), p. 270, no. 24 To be verified with speaker.

So, the wise person will always focus on the hereafter. The Quran says — and a wise person is one who thinks about the hereafter:
يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا كُلَّ أُنَاسٍ بِإِمَامِهِمْ
The Day of Judgment will be that day when every person is called with their Imam. There are a total of 6,600 verses in the Quran, and more than 1,700 of them are about the Day of Judgment.
اللهم صل على محمد وآل محمد وعجل فرجهم

## Preparing for the Hereafter

So, you see how much Allah has emphasized it. In fact, a whole Surah is named after it. Our hadith scholars — Allamah Majlisi (a.r.), in Behaar al-Anwaar, dedicated three and a half volumes entirely to the discussion of *ma'ad* (resurrection and the hereafter): volumes 6, 7, and 8 are entirely devoted to it, and half of volume 5 as well. A person should always think about the Day of Judgment. No one can be sure when they will depart from this world — in one minute a breath ends and the person is gone.

For some people, Allah arranges circumstances for repentance — He may afflict them with illness for months or years. Although in the eyes of the world they are sick, their rational soul is continuously active; they understand everything happening around them but cannot speak. So Allah gives them an opportunity to repent. But many people don't even get this opportunity; suddenly life ends. Therefore, a person must always be prepared.

There are many narrations on this topic. The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) says:
أَكْثِرُوا ذِكْرَ هَادِمِ اللَّذَّاتِ
Remember *hadim al-ladhdhat* — that which destroys pleasures — more and more. The narrator asked, "What is *hadim al-ladhdhat*?" The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) said: "Death." Death is the destroyer of pleasures.

> **Reference:** [Behaar al-Anwaar](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01407/h/263844) (Allamah Majlisi (a.r.)), vol. 70, p. 342, H. 42929

Young people don't quickly grasp these things because when they are young and strong they don't think about it. Ask someone standing near death and they will tell you. Those who have crossed 60 or 70 will say, "What is all this? It passes." The Prophet also says:
أَكْيَسُ النَّاسِ مَنْ أَشَدَّ ذِكْرًا لِلْمَوْتِ
The most intelligent person is one who remembers death the most.

> **Reference:** [Daa'im al-Islam](https://hadith.ahlebaytrepo.com/01464/h/317483) (al-Nu'man al-Maghribi (a.r.)), vol. 1, p. 221, H. 763

And here in the Quran, Allah says:
يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا كُلَّ أُنَاسٍ بِإِمَامِهِمْ
We will call every person with their Imam.

## Choosing One's Imam

Here the Quran gives every human being an invitation to ponder: decide now with whom you will go on the Day of Judgment. With whom will you go? Will you go with the one who says:
أَقِيلُونِي أَقِيلُونِي لَسْتُ بِخَيْرِكُمْ وَعَلِيٌّ فِيكُمْ
"Leave me, leave me! I am not the best among you while Ali (عليه السلام) is among you." Or will you go with the one who, standing on the pulpit, says:
سَلُونِي قَبْلَ أَنْ تَفْقِدُونِي
"Ask me before you lose me."

## The Imamate and Divine Proof

For example, when Imam Reza (عليه السلام) passed away, Imam Jawad (عليه السلام) was in Medina. People there were asking, "Who is the Imam?" A debate arose. One brother stood up claiming the Imamate. All the companions and elders gathered and asked him questions — he knew no answers and was just talking nonsense. All the companions looked at each other in doubt: "He knows nothing."

Then Imam Jawad (عليه السلام) went and delivered a complete sermon, declaring: "You are going here and there, but here I am the proof of Allah — come and ask." Therefore, it is very important to have knowledge of the Imam of the time.

﴿يَوْمَ نَدْعُوا كُلَّ أُنَاسٍ بِإِمَامِهِمْ﴾

Why did Allah use the word 'Imam'? If it had said Prophet or Messenger, Muslims would have an excuse saying, "Our Prophet is the Messenger of Allah; that matter is settled." Allah said 'Imam' so that it becomes clear that this lineage continues until the Day of Judgment. So think about your Imam — who is your Imam? And take everything from your Imam.

## Cultivating Shia Identity in Children

Therefore, we emphasize: bring children to majalis and mahfils. Familiarize children with Shia culture — the manners of a majlis, the manners of a mahfil. Take them to the mosque, tell them all these things so that children understand what our religion is. Take them for Ziyarat if possible. Do you know the basic etiquette of Ziyarat? A young person should recite Ziyarat standing, not sitting — unless it is written in the Ziyarat that 'now you may sit after this.' You are going into the presence of the Imam (عليه السلام).

So teach children all these things. Tell children that one should give charity. Have children give charity with their own hands so that children understand what charity is.

## The Life and Generosity of Ismail ibn Abbad

There was a very great scholar, the late Khunsari. He wrote a book, *Rawdat al-Jannat*, recording the biographies of scholars. In it, he writes about a scholar who was also a minister of Aale Bouyeh dynasty — the same dynasty that was contemporaneous with Shaikh Sadooq (a.r.). His name was Ismail ibn Abbad al-Taleqani; Taleqan is a province in Iran. He was a great scholar and poet, and although his opponents wrote many negative things about him, in the original Shia books there is only praise for him.

Now, Ismail ibn Abbad Taleqani — do you know what habit his parents taught him from childhood? To give charity, and to give it with his own hands. "Make your children give charity with their own hands," says the hadith. When he became a minister, he told his servant: "Every day, place one gold coin and one silver coin under my pillow." It was his habit from childhood and was never missed.

One day he woke up to find no money. The servant said, "My lord, I was busy with work last night and forgot." Now he became very regretful. He told the servant: "Look, whatever furniture is here — all of it — take it all and go give it in charity right now. Don't keep anything. We will get new furniture." The servant asked, "To whom shall we give it?" He had not gone far when he saw a blind Sayyed approaching, weeping, with his daughter beside him. He said, "I am a Sayyed. This is my daughter, and I have no dowry for her." Since the Sayyed could not receive sadaqa, Ismail ibn Abbad said: "Take everything — this is yours, not sadaqa." The Sayyed fainted upon hearing this. When he recovered, Ismail ibn Abbad said: "No, don't take this. I will arrange a complete separate dowry for you. This furniture is for you as a gift." He was known by the title "Sahib" — and let me tell you, this Sahib compiled the book *Uyoon Akhbar al-Ridha* at his request, which Shaikh Sadooq (a.r.) wrote containing the hadith of Imam al-Ridha (عليه السلام). In the month of Ramadan, Ismail ibn Abbad would set a dining cloth for iftar every day where 1,000 people would break their fast. This is the result of teaching charity to children from a young age.

## The Story of Wahab al-Kalbi in Karbala

If you read the incident of Karbala, such people came to help Imam Husayn (عليه السلام) that you will be astonished. A Christian, on his way back from marriage, bringing his sister, his mother, and his wife — one benevolent glance from a Hujjat of God is cast, and he not only becomes a Shia but becomes ready to sacrifice his life. He joins with the Imam.

### The Sacrifice of Wahab

When the Day of Ashura came and the companions started sacrificing one by one, the mother insisted: "Go, my son, sacrifice yourself." The son came and stood before Imam Husayn (عليه السلام) with folded hands: "O Son of the Messenger (صلى الله عليه وآله), grant permission, my Lord." Imam Husayn (عليه السلام) was silent — "How can I give him permission now? He is the sole support of his mother. And he has just brought his wife from her father's house; she hasn't even reached her husband's home yet. How can I give him permission?" The Imam bowed his head. Then a voice came from behind the curtain: "My Lord, grant permission, my Lord." Husayn granted permission. That person's name was Wahab al-Kalbi.

### The Request of the Wife

Now Wahab got ready. As he was about to leave, his wife was standing at the entrance of the tent. He said, "What do you want? Why are you standing?" She said, "I want you to take me to meet the Master once — I will put my request before the Master." He brought her to the Master. She said: "My Lord, I have left my father's house and come, and now he is also going to be martyred. I have no one in this world. Just grant me permission that after his martyrdom I may stay in the tent with the Sayyedahs so that I may be safe."

When Hussain (عليه السلام) heard this, He bowed His head. What could He say now? Walking along the way, they hadn't gone far when footsteps were heard from behind. He said, "O pious woman, why are you coming? I have already bid you farewell." She said: "O brother, when you left, I saw that Hussain (عليه السلام) sat on the ground and was reciting a lamentation."

## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- Believers should strive to outdo one another in good deeds, which means aiming to perform better than others rather than attempting to diminish their efforts.
- The remembrance of Imam Hussain (عليه السلام) should not be limited to mourning periods but should be a daily practice, even within one's prayers.
- Reciting "اللهم رب الحسين بحق الحسين اشف صدر الحسين بظهور الحجة" in prayer, especially during the last prostration, keeps the remembrance of the Imam alive.
- True knowledge is found only in the Quran and the teachings of the Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام); believers should seek religious insights, ethics, and laws exclusively from this source.
- Human intellect is an instinct that can be increased through two primary means: acquiring knowledge and reflecting on one's experiences.
- A believer is characterized by foresight and learning from experience, as evidenced by the principle that one should not be deceived by the same source twice.
- Quality in action is a direct indicator of a person's intellect; one should strive to perform every task, no matter how small, in the best possible manner.
- Prudence (*hazm*) involves waiting for the right opportunity and acting with deliberation, ensuring that one's intentions align with guidance rather than misguidance.
- Humans were created for eternal existence in the hereafter, and a wise person focuses their life and decisions on this ultimate reality.
- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) advised believers to frequently remember death, which he described as the "destroyer of pleasures" (*hadim al-ladhdhat*).
