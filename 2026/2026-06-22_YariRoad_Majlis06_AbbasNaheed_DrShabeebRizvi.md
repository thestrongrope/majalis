## Summary

The lecture focuses on a hadith where the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) addresses Ali (عليه السلام):

يا علي أنت أصل الدين ومنار الإيمان وغاية الهدى وقائد الغر المحجلين
> Reference:

The speaker explains that Ali (عليه السلام) is the foundation of religion, without whom religion cannot exist. ... As the "lighthouse of faith," Ali (عليه السلام) guides believers so their faith does not crash and sink. He is also the ultimate goal of guidance, as supported by the Quranic verse:

إنما أنت منذر ولكل قوم هاد
> Reference:

where the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) identified himself as the warner and Ali (عليه السلام) as the guide. Furthermore, as the leader of the "radiant ones" (الغر المحجلين), Ali (عليه السلام) leads those with completely pure, unadulterated faith. ... The Ahl al-Bayt (عليهم السلام) prioritize pure belief over physical lineage, as demonstrated by Imam al-Sadiq (عليه السلام) stating that his Wilayah of Ali (عليه السلام) is more beloved to him than his physical descent from him.

The speaker presents historical narrations to emphasize the divine decree of Ali's (عليه السلام) Wilayah. A narration from Sheikh Tusi's *Amali* describes how the Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) requested his ring to be engraved with his name, but it miraculously ended up bearing "Muhammad Rasulullah, Ali Waliyyullah," with Angel Jibril (عليه السلام) explaining that Allah wrote what He willed. Another narration from *Ihtijaj al-Tabarsi* refutes altered accounts of the Mi'raj. The Imam (عليه السلام) clarified that when Allah created the Throne, the angels, the heavens, the earth, the sun, and the moon, He inscribed upon them:

لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ مُحَمَّدٌ رَسُولُ اللهِ عَلِيٌّ أَمِيرُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ
> Reference:

The Imam (عليه السلام) then commanded:

فَإِذَا قَالَ أَحَدُكُمْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ مُحَمَّدٌ رَسُولُ اللهِ فَلْيَقُلْ عَلِيٌّ أَمِيرُ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ
> Reference:

The speaker argues that this command indicates that testifying to Ali's (عليه السلام) Wilayah is essential for the acceptance of the testimonies of Tawhid and Prophethood. ... The lecture concludes by referencing Ziyarat Arba'een, noting how those who opposed Ali (عليه السلام) sold their eternal reward for the lowest, cheapest worldly gains.

## Ali is the Foundation of Religion

In the noble hadith chosen for this gathering, the Holy Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) says: "يا علي أنت أصل الدين". "O Ali, you are the foundation of religion, you (عليه السلام) are the root of religion." "أصل الدين". That is, if there is no root, there can be no tree; if there is no foundation, there can be no building. Until there is a foundation, a root, nothing can exist. If there is no Ali, there is no religion. These are the words of the Prophet; ponder upon this, that the Prophet is saying you are the foundation of religion.

So if anyone objects that you people say "Ali, Ali" in everything, they should go and question the Prophet. ...

So the question does not even arise as to why you only mention Ali and why not anyone else? And the second thing is, there is also something called merit and eligibility, brothers. I will present hadiths in your service, and you will see, Inshallah, the matter will become even clearer. First, let me explain this hadith to you.

So there is something called eligibility. When there is no eligibility at all, no eligibility for anything, what is there to mention? So this question of theirs, "Brother, mention them, at least mention them a little." What should we mention? Wherever we mention them, nothing but evil will come up. And if we speak of their evils, that too will feel bad to them. Just like you do Tabarra. Is it clear to you or not? So speaking of evil is also derived from Tabarra.

## The Lighthouse of Faith

"فأنت أصل الدين". "You are the foundation of religion." "ومنار الإيمان". "And the lighthouse of faith." *Minar* means a lighthouse. You know what a lighthouse is, right? It is in the middle of the sea, where there are rocky areas, large stones, and there is a fear that a ship might crash into them. That is why they used to put a light there, so that no ship crashes, and no ship sinks by crashing. That is why they install these lighthouses.

The Commander of the Faithful (عليه السلام) is that lighthouse, so that no one's faith crashes against a rock and sinks.

Subhan Allah. That is why Ali ibn Abi Talib (عليه السلام) is there, so that we may benefit from this light and not crash against any rock. So "منار الإيمان", *minar* is also derived from *noor* (light). *Minar* is derived from *noor*, it is a noun of place (ism al-makan), meaning the place where light is; that is called a *minar*. So the lighthouse of faith is Ali ibn Abi Talib (عليه السلام).

### The Concepts of Hidayah and Ihtida

And Allah has discussed this repeatedly in the Quran, many times in the Quran, and has used two types of words, two concepts. Not just two types of words, but two concepts. One is *hidayah*, meaning to guide, *huda*. And the second is *ihtida*, meaning to receive guidance. So Allah's job is to guide, and the servants' job is to receive guidance. Allah does His work, and the servants must do theirs. Allah has made it clear that Ali ibn Abi Talib is the guide. Believing is the people's job. If someone does not believe, they will only harm themselves. They will not harm Allah, nor Ali, nor the Prophet, nor anyone else. Just as if someone does not believe in Allah, this matter is exactly the same.

And then at the end, the Prophet says: "وقائد الغر المحجلین".

## The Concept of Al-Ghurr al-Muhajjalin

*Muhajjalin*. *Ghurr* refers to a horse whose forehead—for example, if the entire horse is black and its forehead is white, then it is called *ghurr* in Arabic. It is called *ghurr*. This is a sign of purity from one side of the horse, which we in our language call a sign of being a 'Sayyid'. This is the purity of its lineage from one side. From one side of the horse, it is a sign of its being a 'Sayyid', *ghurr*. And *muhajjal* means when its forehead is white and its two hands and feet—all four limbs of the horse—also have whiteness on them. If this is the case, it means this horse is pure from both aspects. That is, it is a 'Sayyid' from the mother's side and also from the father's side. What we in our language call *Najeeb al-Tarfayn* (noble from both sides). This is *Najeeb al-Tarfayn*. From both aspects, it is a completely pure-bred horse. 

The Prophet says you are the leader of *al-Ghurr al-Muhajjalin*. That is, those individuals who are completely pure in their belief in every aspect, in whose belief there is not even an iota of mixture, who do not have the slightest hesitation in their belief. Obviously, what is being discussed here ... is being discussed in terms of belief. Now you might ask, "Just now you gave an example regarding lineage, but you are translating it in terms of belief." So, let us submit to you that what is dearer to the Ahl al-Bayt (علیہم السلام) is belief. Wow, wow, wow, wow! ## The Tragedy of Qasim ibn Hasan

O mourners! Today, the one whose tragedy we have to recite, that child's name is Qasim ibn Hasan (عليه السلام). The martyrs asked this child, "How old are you? What is death to you?" Qasim replied:

أحلى من العسل۔

"Sweeter than honey." ... How much he desires death, martyrdom, how much longing he holds! When Imam Husayn (عليه السلام) was calling out the names of each martyr in the register of martyrdom, he did not call Qasim's name. The child went to a corner, sat down, and began to weep.

### The Amulet of Imam Hasan

While weeping, he suddenly remembered that when his father was leaving this world, he tied an amulet on his arm, and his father had said, "My son, when a very great calamity befalls you, open this amulet and look at it." Qasim thought, "What calamity could be greater than this?" And thinking this, Qasim opened the amulet. As soon as he opened the amulet, there was a writing on it by Imam Hasan (عليه السلام):

بُنَيَّ قَاسِم، أَدْرِكْ عَمَّكَ الْحُسَيْن

"My son Qasim, assist your uncle Husayn."

As soon as the child read this writing, a smile appeared on Qasim's face. Smiling, he came to the presence of Imam Husayn (عليه السلام) and said, "Uncle! Uncle, this is father's writing." As soon as the Imam (عليه السلام) saw the writing, he looked at it and said, "My son Qasim! This is not just your father's writing, but you have brought the message of your own death." Qasim said, "Uncle, it is my father's command that I sacrifice myself for you." Husayn (عليه السلام) said, "Qasim! Oh, tomorrow everyone has to be martyred. I only did not mention your name, otherwise your name was indeed in the register of martyrdom. And Qasim, it was not only your name, but also the name of your brother Ali Asghar."

So while on one hand Qasim was happy to hear his name, that his name was in the register of martyrdom, he writhed in pain upon hearing the name of Ali Asghar. He was a possessor of honor from Bani Hashim, he asked:

يَا عَمِّ! أَلِيَ سِيلُون إِلَى الْخِيَام؟

"O uncle! Will they come to the tents?"

The Imam (عليه السلام) consoled him, "No, the enemies will not enter the tents. I myself will take Ali Asghar to the battlefield." Qasim's heart found peace. I would say, O honorable one of Bani Hashim! ... Sham-e-Ghariban, what a state it was when on the afternoon of Ashura, the enemies entered the tents boldly. They snatched the veils from the heads of the ladies.

### The Martyrdom of Qasim on Ashura

The day of Ashura arrived, the companions were all martyred, and after Zuhr, the martyrdom of Bani Hashim began. Until, at one point, Qasim came before the Imam (عليه السلام) and requested again, "Uncle! Please give me permission, Uncle! Now let me go, everyone is being martyred, Uncle, let me go." Husayn (عليه السلام) remained silent. Husayn (عليه السلام) said nothing. Once, Qasim took both of Husayn's (عليه السلام) hands and began rubbing them on his eyes, weeping bitterly.

Husayn's (عليه السلام) heart overflowed with emotion. Husayn (عليه السلام) gave Qasim permission. The Imam (عليه السلام) gave permission, after which the Imam (عليه السلام) adorned Qasim. He prepared Qasim. He prepared him with his own hands. After preparing him, both the uncle and the nephew embraced each other and wept bitterly. And they wept so much, they wept so much that both of them fainted. O mourners!

When someone faints, water is sprinkled to bring them back to consciousness. But what did Zaynab (عليها السلام) do? She sprinkled tears. She sprinkled tears. When both of them recovered, she said, "Brother, now let Qasim go, do not stop Qasim now." Husayn (عليه السلام) mounted Qasim on his horse. He tore his collar. Qasim asked, "Uncle, what is this?" He replied, "This is the sign of orphans. Perhaps the enemies might feel pity."

"Farewell, Qasim! Now let us see when we meet again." Now it is written in the Maqtal that Qasim fought a tremendous battle and sent more than a hundred enemies to Hell. Then, once, an enemy struck such a blow that Qasim could not balance himself on the horse. This was Qasim's final word, which got stuck. Qasim was hanging half off the horse, half below. The horse became panicked; the horse took Qasim into the midst of the enemies. The enemies began to tear Qasim to pieces. Now when Qasim fell down, the enemies began to trample Qasim's body. As they trampled, they trampled, the horses completely trampled Qasim's body. Qasim called out, "O Imam Husayn! Come and see!" Husayn (عليه السلام) arrived and gathered Qasim's body in a bundle and carried it back.

## Conclusion and Key Takeaways

- The Prophet (صلى الله عليه وآله وسلم) declared to Ali (عليه السلام):
يا علي أنت أصل الدين ومنار الإيمان وغاية الهدى وقائد الغر المحجلين
> Reference:
establishing that Ali (عليه السلام) is the foundation of religion, the lighthouse of faith, the ultimate goal of guidance, and the leader of those with pure faith.

إنما أنت منذر ولكل قوم هاد
